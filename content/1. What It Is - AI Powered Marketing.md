# What It Is — AI-Powered Marketing

## Service Definition

AI-Powered Marketing is a service that introduces AI into every key function of the B2B marketing operation — from CRM data and audience segmentation to content production, competitive monitoring, and campaign reporting — built on HubSpot as the commercial backbone. The service is designed for growth-oriented mid-market companies (€30–100M) in industrial, deep-tech, and B2B technology sectors with complex buying cycles and a marketing team that needs to produce more without growing headcount.

We either build the full system from scratch alongside a HubSpot and Marketing Hub implementation, or layer AI-powered capabilities on top of an existing setup. The result is a marketing operation structured around the Loop Marketing framework — where AI handles data processing, content drafting, distribution, monitoring, and anomaly detection, so the marketing team focuses on strategy, editorial judgment, and the decisions that require human context. Each campaign cycle feeds intelligence back into the next, compounding results over time.

## What AI Does at Each Marketing Function

### CRM Data Analysis

CRM data analysis is the foundation everything else depends on. AI-powered marketing is only as good as the data it runs on — which means before content, campaigns, or reporting, the CRM needs to be clean, complete, and structured in a way that AI can actually use. HubSpot provides a full stack of tools for this.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Data Quality Command Center (Data Hub) — Automatic Data Health Monitoring**

The Data Quality Command Center continuously monitors the CRM for issues and surfaces them in a single dashboard. It detects and helps resolve:

- **Duplicate records** — flags duplicate contacts and companies, allows merging or dismissal; tracks volume of duplicates detected by Breeze over time
- **Formatting inconsistencies** — identifies properties with inconsistent formatting across records (e.g. phone numbers, company names, country fields) and allows auto-correction rules to be set for future entries
- **Enrichment gaps** — scans for missing data across contacts and companies, showing per-property enrichment coverage as a percentage

Marketers can view property fill rates and data health directly inside the report builder — so they know how reliable their data is before making decisions based on it.

**Data Studio (Data Hub) — AI-Assisted Data Blending**

Data Studio is a spreadsheet-like environment inside HubSpot where teams can blend first-party CRM data with third-party data sources — all assisted by AI. Instead of writing formulas or code, marketers can transform and combine data using natural language.

Connects to:
- Google Sheets and Excel (auto-updating)
- Cloud warehouses: Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, AWS S3
- External business tools via integrations

This means marketing data can be combined with finance, product, or customer success data inside HubSpot — giving a complete picture without exporting to separate tools.

**Breeze Intelligence — Automatic Contact & Company Enrichment**

Breeze Intelligence automatically fills in missing data on contact and company records from HubSpot's database of 200M+ company profiles. Marketers get complete audience data without manual research.

*Company-level enrichment:* annual revenue, employee count, industry, technology stack, funding levels, countries of operation

*Contact-level enrichment:* job title, seniority, department, LinkedIn URL, location, time zone

**AI-Powered Audience Segments — Intelligent Audience Discovery**

HubSpot's AI-powered segmentation has evolved beyond static lists. The AI engine:
- **Identifies patterns** across contact and company data that would take weeks to find manually
- **Suggests new segments** continuously based on marketing goals and available data
- **Updates segments in real time** as contact behaviour changes — no manual list maintenance

Four types of segments:
- **CRM-based** — built from contact and company properties
- **Behavioural** — grouped by interactions, interests, and intent signals
- **Web visitor** — targets anonymous visitors based on browsing behaviour, enriched with Breeze Intelligence even before they're known contacts
- **External data** — pulls from integrated business tools

Segments activate directly into email, ads, social, landing pages, and automation workflows through a segment activation library.

**Buyer Intent Data — Identifying In-Market Contacts**

Breeze Intelligence surfaces buyer intent signals — identifying which contacts and companies in the CRM are actively showing purchase intent based on behavioural data. This allows marketers to prioritise outreach and campaigns to the accounts most likely to convert right now.

**Breeze Data Agent — Custom CRM Questions at Scale**

The Breeze Data Agent answers custom questions across the full contact and company database — drawing from CRM records, engagement history, and the web. For marketing, this means being able to ask questions like:
- "Which contacts have engaged with our pricing page but never spoken to sales?"
- "Which companies in our database match our ICP but have no open deals?"
- "Which contacts opened our last three emails but didn't click?"

Results flow back into lists, workflows, and reports — so insights trigger action automatically.

**Loop Marketing Framework — AI-Powered Campaign Intelligence**

HubSpot's Loop Marketing framework is a four-stage AI-powered approach to marketing built around continuous learning from CRM data:

1. **Express** — AI analyses highest-value customers, deal history, and intake form data to define audience profiles and campaign story
2. **Tailor** — behavioural signals, intent data, and CRM context create dynamic audience segments with personalised content for each
3. **Amplify** — content deployed across channels, optimised for search, social, email, and AI answer engines
4. **Evolve** — journey analytics and AI insights feed back into the next loop, making each campaign smarter than the last

The Evolve stage is particularly powerful for CRM data analysis — it connects campaign performance back to contact-level engagement data, so marketers understand not just what worked, but who responded and why.

**Journey Analytics & Pathfinder (Enterprise) — Cross-Channel Attribution**

Pathfinder analyses the paths customers actually take through the marketing journey — identifying where they convert, where they drop off, and which touchpoints drive the most revenue. Connects marketing activity directly to pipeline and closed-won outcomes in the CRM.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Data Quality Command Center configuration** — activating monitoring, setting custom thresholds for duplicate alerts, configuring auto-correction rules for key formatting properties, and establishing a regular data health review cadence
- **Breeze Intelligence activation** — enabling enrichment, setting up auto-enrichment on new contacts, running bulk enrichment on the existing database, and identifying which enriched fields should flow into segmentation and reporting
- **Data Studio setup** — connecting external data sources (Google Sheets, warehouse integrations) and building the first blended datasets relevant to the client's marketing goals
- **Audience segment architecture** — designing the core segment library: which segments are needed (by ICP tier, lifecycle stage, industry, intent level), building them in HubSpot, and connecting them to the segment activation library
- **Buyer intent configuration** — activating buyer intent data and building workflows that act on intent signals (e.g. enrol high-intent contacts in a sequence, alert the sales team)
- **Data Agent configuration** — defining the key CRM questions that matter for this client's marketing strategy and setting up Data Agent queries that trigger automated actions
- **Loop Marketing implementation** — structuring the client's marketing operations around the Loop framework: defining the Express stage inputs (ICP, brand voice), building Tailor-stage segments, and setting up the Evolve-stage analytics loop so each campaign feeds learning back into the next
- **Pathfinder / journey analytics setup** — configuring cross-channel attribution and building the dashboards that connect marketing activity to pipeline outcomes (Enterprise tier)

- Interpreting segment recommendations and deciding which to act on
- Setting marketing goals that guide AI segment suggestions
- Reviewing and approving data corrections before bulk changes
- Deciding which data sources to connect to Data Studio
- Reading journey analytics and drawing strategic conclusions

### Content Pipelines

Content pipelines are where the CRM data analysis work from the previous stage becomes visible. Audience segments, buyer intent signals, and top-performing content insights all feed directly into what gets created, for whom, and across which channels. The goal is to move from ad-hoc content production to a structured, AI-assisted system that creates, personalises, and distributes content at scale — without losing brand quality.

In the Loop Marketing framework, content pipelines sit across the **Express** (create), **Tailor** (personalise), and **Amplify** (distribute) stages. AI handles the execution so the team can focus on strategy and quality.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Brand Voice — The Foundation of All AI Content**

Before any content is generated, HubSpot's brand voice feature is configured. This defines the tone, style, and messaging principles that Breeze uses across every piece of content it creates or suggests — blog posts, emails, social posts, landing pages. All AI-generated content stays consistent with this definition. Available from Marketing Hub Professional.

**Breeze Content Agent — Drafting Across Content Types**

The Breeze Content Agent creates long-form and short-form content directly inside HubSpot. It doesn't start from a blank prompt — it draws from reference files, CRM data, and top-performing content history to produce relevant, on-brand drafts.

What it creates:
- Blog posts — full drafts with topic suggestions based on what's performed well with the target audience
- Landing pages — copy and structure built around campaign objectives
- Case studies — structured using uploaded reference materials and deal/customer data
- Emails — including nurture sequences and campaign emails
- Meta descriptions, internal links, and confirmation emails — automated as pre-publish tasks

It also suggests better blog topics based on top-performing content and target audience data — so the content calendar is informed by what actually resonates, not guesswork.

**Content Remix — One Piece of Content, Many Outputs**

Content Remix is one of the most practical tools in the pipeline. It takes a single piece of existing content — a blog post, landing page, video, PDF, or URL — and remixes it into multiple formats simultaneously, in brand voice.

How it works:
1. Select the source content (upload, URL, or pick from HubSpot)
2. Choose output formats and how many of each to generate: social posts, emails, landing pages, podcast scripts, ads
3. Click Remix — Breeze generates all variations at once
4. Review in the preview panel, then save directly into the relevant HubSpot tool

This turns one strong piece of content into a full multi-channel campaign. A single in-depth article becomes LinkedIn posts, a nurture email, a landing page, and ad copy — all consistent in brand voice, in minutes rather than days.

In the **Loop Marketing Amplify stage**, Content Remix is the engine that scales approved content across every channel without requiring fresh creation for each one.

**Social Media AI Agent — Multi-Channel Social at Scale**

The Breeze Social Media Agent manages the social content pipeline across Facebook, LinkedIn, X, and Instagram. It doesn't just schedule — it analyses and optimises.

What it does:
- Analyses past social performance, brand voice, industry, audience, and best practices to generate post suggestions
- Recommends the best days and times to post for maximum engagement per channel
- Generates customised post variations tailored to each platform's format and audience behaviour

Reps review and approve all posts before anything goes live — the agent drafts and schedules, humans decide what's published. Available on Marketing Hub Professional and Enterprise.

**AEO — Answer Engine Optimization**

Traditional SEO is no longer enough. Buyers increasingly ask AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) before visiting a website — and if a company doesn't appear in those answers, it's invisible at the most critical moment of the buying journey.

HubSpot's AEO tool (launched Spring 2026, currently in beta at $50/month) tracks and improves brand visibility in AI search:

- **Visibility Score** — how often the brand appears across tracked prompts as a percentage
- **Sentiment Analysis** — whether mentions are positive, negative, or neutral (-100 to +100 scale)
- **Share of Voice** — brand mentions vs. competitors across identical queries
- **Citation Analysis** — which pages, domains, and content types appear in AI answers
- **Content recommendations** — specific actions to take: create a new page, update existing content, publish social posts, or reach out to third-party publications

AEO turns AI answer engine visibility into a content brief — telling the team exactly what to create to appear more often in the places buyers are actually looking. This feeds directly back into the content pipeline: AEO insights → content creation → Content Remix → distribution.

**AI Email Content Generation**

Breeze Assistant generates email copy for campaigns, nurture sequences, and one-to-one outreach directly inside HubSpot's email editor. It adapts tone and messaging to the recipient's lifecycle stage and segment — so a first-touch email reads differently from a re-engagement email to a lapsed contact.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Brand voice definition** — working with the client to define and configure their brand voice in HubSpot so every piece of AI-generated content is consistent from day one
- **Breeze Content Agent configuration** — uploading reference files (existing content, product docs, messaging guides), connecting top-performing content data, and tuning topic suggestions to the client's ICP and content goals

- **Email content templates** — building AI-assisted email templates per lifecycle stage, segment, and use case inside HubSpot's email editor
- **Content calendar automation** — connecting the content pipeline to HubSpot's campaign tools so content creation, remix, scheduling, and publishing run as a coordinated system rather than separate manual tasks

#### What the Marketing Team Still Does

- Setting the content strategy and deciding which topics and themes to pursue
- Reviewing and approving all AI-generated content before publication
- Providing source material — interviews, product updates, customer stories — that AI can work from

### Competitive Monitoring

Competitive monitoring in B2B marketing serves two purposes: understanding where you stand relative to competitors in the market, and surfacing competitor intelligence from inside your own CRM — what prospects are saying about alternatives, which competitors come up in calls, and which accounts might be evaluating other vendors. HubSpot covers both, though with different depths.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**AEO — AI Search Competitive Monitoring**

The most significant competitive monitoring capability HubSpot now offers is through its AEO tool. As AI search engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) become a primary discovery channel for B2B buyers, who gets cited in AI answers is becoming as strategically important as search ranking.

HubSpot's AEO tool tracks competitive positioning across AI answer engines:

- **Share of Voice** — your brand mentions as a percentage of all brand mentions (yours + competitors) across tracked prompts. Calculated consistently so changes over time are comparable.
- **Sentiment analysis** — whether AI tools describe your brand positively, negatively, or neutrally (-100 to +100 scale), and how that compares to competitors across the same queries
- **Presence quality & brand recognition** — how prominently and consistently your brand is cited vs. competitors
- **Citation analysis** — which sources, pages, and domains AI tools draw from when answering questions about your category. Reveals which competitor content is earning AI citations you aren't.
- **Competitor comparison** — any brand can be analysed using the same scoring framework, enabling direct side-by-side benchmarking

The tool queries ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity simultaneously and produces a composite score across five dimensions. Reports are exportable for tracking over time.

Critically, the tool converts competitive gaps into a content action list — specific recommendations for what to create to close the share of voice gap against named competitors.

In the **Loop Marketing Evolve stage**, AEO competitive data feeds directly back into the next content cycle: where competitors are being cited instead of you becomes the brief for the next content sprint.

**Breeze Data Agent — Competitor Intelligence from Your Own CRM**

The Breeze Data Agent can answer custom competitive questions across the full CRM — pulling from contact records, email history, call transcripts, and notes:

- "Which prospects mentioned a competitor in our emails or calls?"
- "Which accounts are we losing to [competitor name] and what reasons were given?"
- "Which open deals have competitor names mentioned in their notes?"

Results flow back into lists and workflows — so competitive signals from the CRM trigger automated actions (e.g. alert the sales team, enrol in a competitor-specific nurture sequence).

**Conversation Intelligence — Competitor Mentions in Calls**

Sales call transcripts (captured via HubSpot's Conversation Intelligence) are automatically analysed for competitor mentions. Marketing teams can use this data to:
- Understand which competitors come up most frequently at which deal stages
- Identify the objections and comparison points that need addressing in content
- Feed competitive messaging gaps back into the content pipeline

**Buyer Intent Data — Identifying Competitive Evaluation Signals**

Breeze Intelligence buyer intent signals can flag when contacts and companies in the CRM are showing research behaviour that may indicate competitor evaluation — enabling proactive outreach before a decision is made.

#### The Gap — and How We Fill It

HubSpot's native competitive monitoring is strong on AI search visibility and CRM-sourced intelligence, but it doesn't cover external competitive intelligence: competitor website changes, new product launches, pricing updates, job postings that signal strategic shifts, or social media monitoring.

- Web monitoring for competitor content and announcement pages

Findings are structured and fed into HubSpot as CRM notes, tasks, or contact/company properties — so all competitive intelligence lives in one place and can trigger marketing workflows.

- **Buyer intent competitive workflows** — building automations that act on intent signals suggesting competitor evaluation

### Campaign Reporting & Analysis

Campaign reporting and analysis is where the Loop Marketing framework closes the loop. CRM data analysis provides the audience, content pipelines produce and distribute the material, competitive monitoring tracks where the brand stands — and campaign reporting connects all of it back to outcomes. The goal is to move from manual report-building and gut-feel interpretation to an AI-assisted system that surfaces what worked, what didn't, and what to do next — automatically.

In the Loop Marketing framework, campaign reporting is the **Evolve** stage: every campaign becomes a structured learning cycle that feeds intelligence back into the next Express stage. AI handles anomaly detection, performance summarisation, and attribution — so the team spends time on decisions, not data assembly.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Breeze Assistant in Reporting — Natural Language Report Building**

Inside HubSpot's report builder, Breeze Assistant allows marketers to generate reports through natural language questions rather than manual configuration. Instead of navigating filters and data structures, a marketer can ask:

- "Show me campaign performance by channel for Q3"
- "Which landing pages generated the most qualified contacts this quarter?"
- "Compare email click-through rates across our last five campaigns"

Breeze builds the report template, applies recommended filters and visualisations, and the marketer saves or edits from there. Reports can be added directly to dashboards.

Breeze Assistant also hovers over existing reports to surface main takeaways — providing a plain-language summary of what a report shows so users don't have to interpret raw charts manually.

**AI-Generated Campaign Summaries — Instant Performance Overviews**

From any campaign details page, Breeze generates an AI summary of campaign performance. This includes an overview of key metrics, identification of top-performing assets, and follow-up questions the marketer can ask about the data. This makes campaign reviews faster — the summary surfaces the headline insights without requiring someone to dig through individual asset reports.

**AI Email Performance Summaries — Post-Send Analysis with Recommendations**

After each email send, HubSpot generates an AI-powered performance summary that goes beyond standard metrics. It:

- Spots anomalies in open rates, click rates, and deliverability compared to historical baseline
- Identifies what's working and what isn't
- Surfaces specific recommendations: subject line changes, send-time adjustments, segment refinements
- Includes deliverability health scores and engagement decay triggers

Crucially, the benchmarks used are drawn from the account's own audience behaviour — not industry averages — so the analysis is relevant to the specific list and content, not generic norms.

**Automated Anomaly Detection — Performance Monitoring Without Manual Watching**

HubSpot's AI monitors campaigns for unusual shifts and flags them automatically, without requiring manual dashboards to be watched. It detects:

- Unexpected dips or spikes in email engagement, conversion rates, or traffic
- Pipeline-stage anomalies — leads moving slower or faster than normal patterns suggest
- Campaign ROI deviations from expected trajectory

Alerts are surfaced in-platform so marketing teams are notified of issues in time to act, rather than discovering them in a monthly review.

**Campaign Goals Tracker — Structured Goal Tracking with Real-Time Progress**

The Campaign Goals Tracker provides a structured way to set measurable campaign objectives and track them in real time. Goal types include new contacts generated, SQLs, website traffic, cost per customer, and influenced revenue. Goals can be set on a single, monthly, quarterly, or yearly basis — and multiple goals can run simultaneously on a single campaign (e.g., "generate 200 new contacts" and "influence €50k revenue").

Progress is tracked automatically as associated assets — emails, landing pages, social posts, ads — generate data. The tracker aggregates performance across all assets and shows live progress against each goal, eliminating the need to manually compile data at the end of a campaign.

Available on Marketing Hub Professional and Enterprise.

**Multi-Touch Attribution Reporting — Connecting Marketing Activity to Revenue**

HubSpot's attribution reporting assigns credit to the marketing touchpoints that contribute to contact creation and, at Enterprise tier, to deal creation and closed-won revenue. Six attribution models are available:

- **First-touch** — all credit to the first interaction; useful for understanding awareness drivers
- **Last-touch** — all credit to the final interaction before conversion; useful for identifying conversion triggers
- **Linear** — equal credit across all touchpoints in the journey
- **U-shaped** — 40% to first and last touches, 20% distributed across middle interactions; useful for B2B lead generation
- **W-shaped** — 30% each to first touch, lead creation, and opportunity creation; 10% to remaining interactions; the most B2B-aligned model
- **Time decay** — more credit to recent touchpoints; useful for shorter buying cycles

Attribution reports show which campaigns, channels, content types, and specific assets contributed to each outcome — so the team can redirect budget and effort toward what's actually generating pipeline.

Contact creation attribution is available at Professional tier. Full revenue attribution (deal creation + closed-won revenue) requires Enterprise.

**Journey Analytics & Pathfinder — Mapping the Paths That Drive Conversion**

Pathfinder analyses the actual paths contacts take through the marketing funnel — not the paths marketers assume they take. It automatically identifies patterns: which touchpoints consistently precede deal closure, where contacts drop off, and which sequences of interactions correlate with the highest conversion rates.

Where traditional funnel reports show volume at each stage, Pathfinder shows the routes — making it possible to understand not just how many contacts convert, but which journey patterns produce the best results.

Connects directly to pipeline and closed-won outcomes in the CRM, making it the clearest view of how marketing activity translates to revenue. Available on Marketing Hub Professional and Enterprise.

**Breeze Data Agent — Custom Performance Questions at Scale**

The Breeze Data Agent answers custom analytics questions across the full CRM and campaign history — drawing from contact records, engagement data, campaign results, and the web. For campaign reporting, this means being able to ask:

- "Which campaigns generated the most pipeline-stage contacts in the last six months?"
- "Which email sequences have the highest conversion rate from MQL to SQL?"
- "Which content assets are most often the last touchpoint before deal creation?"

Results flow back into reports, lists, and workflows — so performance insights can trigger automatic actions rather than sitting in a dashboard waiting to be reviewed.

**Loop Marketing Evolve Stage — Making Every Campaign Smarter Than the Last**

The Evolve stage is where campaign reporting becomes campaign intelligence. Rather than reviewing results and starting fresh, the Evolve stage feeds performance data back into the next campaign cycle:

- Journey analytics and attribution data inform which segments, messages, and assets to prioritise in the next Express stage
- AEO data identifies where the brand is losing visibility to competitors, feeding the next content sprint
- Anomaly detection and email performance summaries surface issues before they compound
- A/B testing results across channels (headlines, offers, images, audiences) feed into creative decisions for the next Tailor stage

The result is that each campaign cycle produces not just outcomes but learnings — and those learnings compound. The second campaign is smarter than the first, the third smarter than the second. This is the structural advantage of the Loop framework over one-off campaign execution.

- **Attribution model selection and setup** — assessing the client's sales cycle, deal stages, and reporting needs to recommend the appropriate attribution model, configuring it in HubSpot, and building the initial attribution reports that connect marketing activity to pipeline and revenue

- Interpreting attribution reports and deciding where to shift budget and effort
- Running the Evolve review sessions and translating insights into campaign briefs

## What This Is Not

- **Not a HubSpot implementation project** — HubSpot setup is often part of the engagement, but the service is a marketing intelligence and execution system built on top of it, not a CRM deployment. The outcome is a running marketing operation, not a configured platform.
- **Not a content agency** — we set up the AI-powered content pipeline and configure the system that produces content. We are not a creative agency writing blog posts and social updates on an ongoing retainer. The AI generates drafts; the client's team reviews and publishes. Our role is building the infrastructure that makes that possible at scale.
- **Not a paid media or campaign management service** — we integrate ad performance data into attribution and reporting, but we do not run Google Ads, Meta campaigns, or paid media on behalf of clients. The service is about owned and earned channels and the intelligence layer across all of them.
- **Not traditional SEO** — AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is a distinct discipline from Google search ranking. Appearing in AI answer engines requires different content strategies and different signals than ranking in traditional search. The service addresses both, but clients should understand they are different problems requiring different approaches.
- **Not a one-off project** — the system requires ongoing monitoring, tuning, and iteration. Segments go stale, AEO rankings shift, campaign learnings need to feed back into the next cycle. A single implementation without a retainer will degrade. The value compounds over time through the Loop Marketing Evolve stage — not on day one.
- **Not a tool that replaces the marketing team** — AI handles data processing, content drafting, distribution, monitoring, and anomaly detection. The marketing team still owns strategy, editorial judgment, creative direction, and the decisions that require human context. The goal is to remove operational drag and amplify what the team is already capable of.
