# How We Deliver It — AI-Powered Marketing

## Engagement Model

Every engagement ends up in the same place — an AI-powered marketing system running on HubSpot with an ongoing retainer to keep it sharp. How we get there depends on the customer's starting point.

**Path A — Full implementation (new to HubSpot or migrating)**
A single integrated project covering HubSpot and Marketing Hub setup and AI-powered marketing configuration together. Delivered in phases over 6–10 weeks, followed by a monthly retainer. The CRM and Marketing Hub are the foundation; the AI marketing system is built on top from day one.

**Path B — Already on HubSpot**
Flexible entry points depending on the customer's readiness:
- Audit only → clear assessment of current Marketing Hub setup and a roadmap, no further commitment required
- Audit + Pilot → prove value on 1–2 marketing functions before full commitment
- Direct to full build → for customers who are ready and have a solid HubSpot and Marketing Hub foundation

All paths converge on the same destination: AI-powered marketing across all four functions — CRM data, content pipelines, competitive monitoring, and campaign reporting — maintained on a monthly retainer.

**Ongoing retainer**
The system is not a set-and-forget build. Audience segments go stale as contact behaviour changes, AEO recommendations need acting on, content pipelines need reviewing, and the Evolve stage requires regular sessions to feed campaign learnings back into planning. The retainer is what keeps the system performing — typically 10–30 hours per month depending on scope.

## Tools & Technology

**Core platform**
- **HubSpot** — Marketing Hub and Smart CRM are the operational backbone. All AI marketing features run within HubSpot.

**HubSpot AI (Breeze)**
- **Breeze Content Agent** — long-form and short-form content drafting: blog posts, landing pages, case studies, emails
- **Content Remix** — converts a single piece of content into multiple formats and channels simultaneously
- **Social Media Agent** — AI-powered social content generation, scheduling, and optimisation across LinkedIn, Facebook, X, and Instagram
- **Brand Voice** — defines tone, style, and messaging so all AI-generated content stays consistent
- **AEO tool** — tracks brand visibility, share of voice, sentiment, and citation analysis across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity
- **Breeze Intelligence** — automatic contact and company enrichment from HubSpot's 200M+ company database
- **Breeze Data Agent** — custom research and analytics questions across the full CRM and campaign history
- **Breeze Assistant** — natural language report generation, campaign summaries, and AI-generated insights in dashboards
- **AI-Powered Audience Segments** — real-time updating segments based on CRM, behavioural, and intent data
- **Buyer Intent Data** — surfaces contacts and companies showing in-market purchase signals
- **Data Quality Command Center** — continuous CRM health monitoring, duplicate detection, and enrichment gap tracking
- **Data Studio** — AI-assisted data blending across HubSpot, Google Sheets, Excel, and cloud warehouses
- **Journey Analytics / Pathfinder** — maps customer paths through the funnel and connects touchpoints to pipeline outcomes
- **Campaign Goals Tracker** — structured goal setting and real-time progress tracking across campaign assets
- **Multi-touch attribution reporting** — six attribution models connecting marketing activity to contact creation and closed-won revenue
- **Conversation Intelligence** — call transcription and competitive mention tracking for marketing intelligence

**External monitoring**
- **make.com** — building automated flows that track competitor website changes, press releases, and social activity and feed structured intelligence back into HubSpot where native monitoring has gaps

**AI development**
- **Claude Code** — used internally for building custom automations, data processing, and any bespoke AI logic that goes beyond what HubSpot's native tools provide

## Team & Roles

A typical engagement involves four roles, sometimes carried by two or three people depending on scope:

**Marketing strategist / consultant**
Owns the commercial and content strategy design. Works with the client to define ICP and audience segment architecture, brand voice, content strategy, Loop Marketing framework structure, and what "good" looks like across each marketing function. This person understands the client's business and translates it into HubSpot and AI configuration decisions.

**HubSpot specialist**
Owns the technical implementation. Responsible for Marketing Hub configuration, CRM data architecture, contact properties, lifecycle stages, workflow builds, campaign setup, attribution model configuration, and ensuring the HubSpot foundation is solid enough for the AI layer to run on top.

**AI & content specialist**
Configures the Breeze content tools — Content Agent, Content Remix, Social Media Agent, Brand Voice — and sets up AEO monitoring, competitive workflows, audience segments, and any make.com automation flows. Responsible for the quality and consistency of AI-generated content outputs and the integrity of the content pipeline.

**Account lead (retainer)**
Owns the ongoing relationship. Runs the Evolve stage review sessions, monitors system performance, identifies what needs tuning, manages the monthly retainer hours, and brings new HubSpot or AI capabilities to the client's attention as the platform evolves. May be the same person as the strategist.

## Process

### Path A — Full Implementation

**Week 1–2: Kickoff & discovery**
- Kickoff meeting with key stakeholders (marketing lead, CRM admin, sales representative)
- Deep-dive into current marketing operation: content production, channels, team structure, reporting, ICP
- Brand voice workshop: defining tone, messaging principles, and style guidelines that will govern all AI-generated content
- Access provisioning: HubSpot account, social accounts, ad accounts, data sources
- Content audit: reviewing existing assets that will serve as reference material for the Content Agent

**Week 2–4: HubSpot & Marketing Hub build**
- CRM data architecture: contact and company properties, lifecycle stages, lead status definitions
- Marketing Hub setup: email domains, tracking configuration, form setup, landing page framework
- Core integrations connected: website tracking, social accounts, ad platforms
- Data Studio connections configured where external data sources are in scope

**Week 4–7: AI layer build**
- Data Quality Command Center activated: duplicate monitoring, enrichment gap tracking, formatting rules configured
- Breeze Intelligence activated: bulk enrichment run on existing database, auto-enrichment enabled for new contacts
- Audience segment architecture built: core segments by ICP tier, lifecycle stage, intent level, industry — connected to the segment activation library
- Buyer intent data activated and intent-triggered workflows built
- Brand voice configured in HubSpot
- Content Agent set up: reference files uploaded, top-performing content connected, topic suggestions tuned to ICP
- Content Remix workflow established: core content assets identified, remix outputs configured per channel
- Social Media Agent connected and configured: accounts linked, posting cadence established, first batch reviewed
- AEO tool configured: buyer prompts defined, competitors added for benchmarking, initial share of voice report run
- Competitive monitoring set up: Data Agent competitive queries defined, Conversation Intelligence competitor tagging enabled, make.com competitor monitoring flows built where needed
- Campaign Goals Tracker configured: goal types, frequencies, and asset associations set up
- Attribution model selected and reports built: appropriate model for the client's sales cycle, dashboards connecting marketing to pipeline
- Pathfinder / journey analytics configured: key funnel stages defined, journey reports built
- Data Agent campaign queries defined and connected to automated actions
- Loop Marketing Evolve stage cadence established: review session schedule, brief template, feedback-to-planning process

**Week 7–8: Testing & training**
- Full system walkthrough with the marketing team
- First AI-generated content reviewed together — blog post, email, social posts — and refined against brand voice
- Test run of Content Remix on a real asset
- Marketing team trained on: content pipeline, segment management, AEO review, campaign dashboard, Evolve review process
- Final adjustments before go-live

**Week 8+: Go-live & retainer**
- System live across all four marketing functions
- Monthly retainer begins: monitoring, segment updates, AEO action items, Evolve stage reviews, and content pipeline tuning

### Path B — Audit

**Week 1: Audit**
- Access to HubSpot and Marketing Hub account
- Assessment of: contact data quality, existing segments, which AI features are active and which are unused, Marketing Hub configuration, email performance, attribution setup, content asset library
- Output: a clear written assessment and a prioritised roadmap for AI-powered marketing

**Deliverable:** HubSpot Marketing Hub audit report + AI-powered marketing roadmap

---

### Path B — Pilot

**Week 1: Scoping**
- Based on audit findings (or discovery if no audit), agree on which 1–2 marketing functions are in scope for the pilot
- Define success metrics for the pilot period

**Week 2–3: Build**
- Configure the AI layer for the agreed functions only
- Marketing team training on the specific tools in scope

**Weeks 3–12: Run & measure**
- System runs for 90 days
- Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins to review content output quality, segment performance, and early results
- Results documented against the agreed success metrics

**End of pilot: Review & proposal**
- Pilot debrief with stakeholders
- Results presented against success metrics
- Proposal for full build across remaining functions

---

### Path B — Full Build (post-audit or post-pilot)

Follows the same AI layer build and go-live process as Path A weeks 4–8, scoped to the functions not yet in place.

## Onboarding a New Client

The first two weeks set the tone for the whole engagement. The goal is to move fast enough to show momentum, and thorough enough to build something that actually works.

**Day 1: Access & alignment**
- HubSpot and Marketing Hub access granted to the delivery team
- Social and ad account access shared
- Kickoff meeting booked with all key stakeholders
- Delivery timeline shared and agreed

**Week 1: Kickoff meeting**
The kickoff covers:
- The client's marketing operation end-to-end — how they currently produce content, what channels they use, how campaigns are run and reported on
- Their ICP and audience in detail — who they're marketing to, how segments are currently defined, what content has performed best
- CRM situation — what's in HubSpot, what's missing, what the marketing team actually uses
- Brand voice — initial conversation on tone, style, and messaging principles that will guide all AI-generated content
- What success looks like in 90 days

**Week 1–2: Foundations**
- Data quality check run on the existing HubSpot contact and company database
- Bulk enrichment run with Breeze Intelligence to fill critical gaps before segmentation begins
- Audience segment architecture drafted and validated with the client
- Brand voice defined and configured in HubSpot
- Existing content assets catalogued and uploaded as reference material for the Content Agent

**First milestone: First AI-powered content approved and live**
The first visible output is a piece of AI-generated content — a blog post draft or a set of social posts — reviewed, approved, and published by the client's team. This is typically achievable within 2–3 weeks of kickoff and gives the client an early, tangible signal that the system is producing real output — not just configuration in the background.
