# How We Prove It Works — AI-Powered Marketing

## Key Metrics

Proof needs to be structured at two levels: operational metrics that show the system is running well, and outcome metrics that connect marketing activity to business results. Both matter — operational metrics are visible early, outcome metrics take longer to build but are what the client ultimately cares about.

### Operational metrics — the system is working

**Content pipeline**
- Content output volume: pieces published per month vs. before the engagement
- Time from brief to published content: how long each content type takes end-to-end
- Content Remix throughput: how many channel outputs are generated from each core asset
- Brand voice consistency: whether AI-generated content is passing review without major revisions

**Data & segmentation**
- Contact enrichment rate: % of contacts with key fields populated (industry, title, company size, intent signals)
- Segment accuracy: whether contacts in a segment actually match the criteria
- Data health score: duplicate rate and formatting consistency in the CRM (tracked in Data Quality Command Center)
- Segment activation: how many active segments are connected to live marketing workflows

**Social & AEO**
- Social posting consistency: posts per week per channel vs. target cadence
- AEO visibility score: % of tracked buyer prompts where the brand appears
- Share of voice trend: brand mentions vs. competitors over time across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity
- Citation sources: which pages and domains are being cited in AI answers

### Outcome metrics — the system is driving results

**Engagement**
- Email open rate and click-through rate vs. pre-engagement baseline
- Landing page conversion rate
- Social engagement rate per post vs. pre-engagement baseline

**Pipeline influence**
- % of closed-won deals with at least one marketing touchpoint attributed
- Number of MQLs generated per month vs. pre-engagement baseline
- Time from first marketing touchpoint to MQL

**Revenue connection**
- Attributed revenue: closed-won deal value connected to marketing touchpoints via attribution reporting
- Cost per MQL and cost per influenced deal
- Pipeline influenced by marketing campaigns in the reporting period

**Compounding improvement (Loop Marketing Evolve)**
- Campaign performance trend across cycles: are later campaigns outperforming earlier ones?
- AEO visibility score trend over 3, 6, and 12 months
- Segment conversion rates improving as targeting becomes more accurate over time

## Feedback Loop — Pilots to Service Design

Every engagement, including early pilots, generates learnings that should feed back into how the service is designed and delivered. The goal is to build a feedback loop between client work and the service definition itself — the same Evolve-stage logic we apply in the service applies to how we run the service.

**What to capture from every engagement:**
- Which marketing functions had the most immediate visible impact — content pipeline, AEO, segmentation, or attribution?
- Which configuration decisions took longer than expected and why?
- Which parts of the system the client's marketing team found easiest to operate independently, and which required ongoing support?
- Which HubSpot AI features performed as expected and which needed workarounds?
- What questions or objections came up during the engagement that weren't anticipated in the sales process?
- What did the Evolve stage review sessions reveal about the Loop framework in practice?

**How learnings get used:**
- Process adjustments: if a configuration step consistently takes longer than planned, the delivery timeline is updated
- Scope adjustments: if a particular function consistently delivers faster results, it becomes the recommended pilot entry point
- Messaging adjustments: if a specific proof point or outcome resonates strongly with clients, it gets incorporated into sales conversations
- Service additions: if a gap keeps appearing (a tool that HubSpot doesn't cover, a client need we keep seeing), it becomes a candidate for adding to the service stack

## How We Talk About Results Externally

**Lead with pipeline, not output**
The most common mistake in marketing reporting is leading with content volume, social followers, or email open rates. These are operational signals. The conversation that earns trust — especially with commercial leaders — is about pipeline: how many of the deals we closed had marketing touchpoints? What revenue did marketing influence? This is what attribution reporting makes possible, and it should be the primary external narrative.

**Show the trend, not just the snapshot**
Single-point metrics are easy to dismiss. A trend — AEO visibility improving from 12% to 34% over six months, or campaign-influenced pipeline doubling across three Loop cycles — is harder to argue with. Lead with direction and momentum, not just a number at a point in time.

**Be specific about what AI does vs. what the team does**
In external conversations, clarity about the division of labour matters. AI handles drafting, monitoring, distributing, and detecting anomalies. The marketing team handles strategy, approval, and editorial judgment. The result is more output, better targeting, and clearer attribution — not AI doing marketing autonomously. This framing is more credible and avoids the scepticism that comes with overclaiming.

**Use the Loop Marketing Evolve stage as the proof of compounding**
The most distinctive claim of the service is that it compounds — each campaign makes the next one smarter. When talking externally, the Evolve stage is the mechanism that makes this claim concrete: campaign data feeds back into segment design, AEO data feeds the content brief, attribution data redirects budget. The narrative is not "we ran a campaign and got results" — it's "the system learned from the campaign and the next one performed better."

**Honest about what's still being learned**
This is a new service built on AI capabilities that are evolving fast. Being honest about that — rather than overpromising — is a competitive advantage with sophisticated buyers who have seen AI hype before. The right framing: we know the framework, we know the platform, we are actively learning what works best for which types of client — and that learning gets applied directly to every engagement.
