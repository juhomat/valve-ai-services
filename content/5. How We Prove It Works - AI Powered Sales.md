# How We Prove It Works — AI-Powered Sales

## Key Metrics

Proof needs to be structured at two levels: operational metrics that show the system is running well, and outcome metrics that connect the sales system to business results. Operational metrics are visible early. Outcome metrics take longer to build but are what commercial leaders ultimately care about.

### Operational metrics — the system is working

**Prospecting**
- Contacts enrolled in the Prospecting Agent per week
- Buying signals detected and acted on per month
- Outreach sent — volume and response rate on AI-drafted emails vs. manually written baseline
- Contact enrichment coverage: % of contacts with complete key fields (title, company size, phone, LinkedIn)

**Research & qualification**
- Lead scoring coverage: % of contacts and companies with an active score
- Score accuracy: % of contacts scoring above threshold that convert to meetings
- Company Research Agent usage: how often reps are running pre-call briefs before meetings
- Time from contact created to qualified: average days, vs. pre-engagement baseline

**CRM hygiene**
- Smart Deal Progression adoption: % of deal records updated within 24 hours of a call
- Deal stage accuracy: proportion of deals in a stage that match the actual conversation history
- Forecast confidence: whether AI forecast aligns with rep-reported pipeline

**Offer building**
- Quote turnaround time: from deal stage to sent quote, before vs. after CPQ
- Quote approval time: average time in approval workflow
- Quote-to-close rate: % of sent quotes that convert to signed deals

### Outcome metrics — the system is driving results

**Rep time**
- Hours saved per rep per week on admin — CRM updates, research, writing (estimated from rep feedback and call volume data)
- Ratio of selling time to admin time, before vs. after engagement

**Pipeline**
- Pipeline velocity: average time in each deal stage vs. pre-engagement baseline
- Conversion rates at each stage: MQL → SQL → meeting booked → proposal sent → closed won
- Number of active deals per rep — are reps managing more pipeline with the same time?

**Revenue**
- Win rate: % of deals closed won vs. pre-engagement baseline
- Average deal size: before vs. after CPQ and AI-assisted quoting
- Forecast accuracy: variance between AI forecast and actual closed-won revenue at quarter end

## Feedback Loop — Pilots to Service Design

Every engagement generates learnings that should feed back into how the service is designed and delivered. The goal is to build a structured feedback loop between client work and the service definition itself.

**What to capture from every engagement:**
- Which of the five sales stages had the most immediate visible impact — where did reps notice the difference first?
- Which configuration decisions took longer than expected and why?
- Which parts of the system the sales team adopted fastest vs. which required more coaching?
- Which HubSpot AI features performed as expected and which needed workarounds or supplementary tooling?
- What rep objections or concerns came up during training that weren't anticipated?
- How accurately did the lead scoring model perform against real outcomes in the first 90 days?
- What questions or objections came up in sales conversations that weren't addressed in the current messaging?

**How learnings get used:**
- Process adjustments: if a configuration step consistently takes longer than planned, the delivery timeline is updated
- Scope adjustments: if a particular stage consistently delivers the fastest visible results, it becomes the recommended pilot entry point
- Messaging adjustments: if a specific proof point resonates strongly with prospects, it gets incorporated into sales conversations
- Service additions: if a gap keeps appearing — a need the current stack doesn't cover — it becomes a candidate for adding to the service

## How We Talk About Results Externally

**Lead with rep time, not feature lists**
The most compelling claim is simple: reps spend more time selling and less time on admin. That's the lived experience that resonates — not which Breeze agent is configured or how the scoring model is calibrated. The conversation should start with the admin-to-selling ratio: how many hours per week does a rep currently spend on research, CRM updates, and writing vs. actually talking to prospects? Then show how that changes.

**Make pipeline outcomes concrete**
Beyond time savings, the commercial case is pipeline velocity and win rate. Deals moving faster through stages, fewer deals stalling after meetings, more accurate forecasting. When talking to a sales leader or CRO, these are the numbers that matter. Attribution to revenue is the ultimate proof — but pipeline velocity is visible faster and is a credible leading indicator.

**Use the five-stage frame as the proof structure**
The five-stage structure (Prospecting → Research → Preparation → Offer → Closing) is useful externally because it maps directly to how sales leaders think about their own process. Walking through each stage and showing what changes — concretely, not abstractly — makes the service feel tangible rather than theoretical. "At the prospecting stage, your reps stop writing cold emails manually. At the meeting prep stage, they walk into every call with a briefing that took zero time to produce." That specificity is more persuasive than any headline metric.

**Show before and after, not just the after**
The strongest external proof is a comparison: what the process looked like before, and what it looks like now. Time per task, conversion rates at each stage, forecast accuracy. A single data point is easy to dismiss. A before-and-after comparison at multiple stages is harder to argue with.

**Honest about what's still being learned**
This is a new service built on AI capabilities that are evolving fast. Being honest about that — rather than overpromising — is a competitive advantage with sophisticated commercial buyers who have seen AI hype before. The right framing: we know the framework, we know the platform, we are actively learning what works best for which types of sales team — and that learning gets applied directly to every engagement.
