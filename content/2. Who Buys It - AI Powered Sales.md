# Who Buys It — AI-Powered Sales

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Growth-Oriented Mid-Market — €30–100M revenue**

Industrial, deep-tech, or B2B technology companies with:

- **50–500 employees**
- **International growth ambitions** — operating or expanding across 2–5 markets
- **6–18 month sales cycles** — complex deals with multiple stakeholders, not transactional
- A **structured sales team** already in place — not solely CEO-led selling
- A **marketing owner or small team** investing in commercial maturity
- Entering a **next growth phase** — scaling pipeline, markets, or headcount
- **HubSpot already in place** OR actively migrating from Salesforce, Pardot, or a legacy CRM

**Why this profile is the right fit:**
- Large enough to invest in a serious commercial system
- Small enough to move fast and make decisions without six months of internal approvals
- Strong enough growth ambitions that sales efficiency becomes a real constraint
- High potential impact from structured CRM, AI-powered processes, and a proper operating model

**Example customer types:**
- Industrial tech companies modernising their go-to-market
- Professional services firms productising their expertise into a scalable sales process
- Climate/energy tech scale-ups expanding internationally

**Named examples from our client base:** Stora Enso, Virta, Abax, Katana

**Two types of buyer — same ICP, different starting point:**
- **Already on HubSpot** — the CRM is in place but the sales process is still largely manual. AI is layered on top of what exists.
- **New to HubSpot or migrating** — the CRM move and the AI-powered sales build happen together as one integrated engagement.

The sales motion differs between the two (see How We Sell It), but the ICP profile is the same.

## The Pain They Feel

These companies have a sales team and a pipeline — but the process is held together manually. Common pain points that make them ready for AI-Powered Sales:

- **Current CRM isn't working** — they're on Salesforce, Pardot, or a legacy system that the team has stopped trusting or actively using. They know they need to move but want the next platform to actually stick. *(Path A buyers)*
- **Reps spend too much time on admin** — researching accounts, writing emails, updating CRM fields — instead of selling
- **Inconsistent outreach quality** — prospecting and follow-up depends on who the rep is and how much time they have
- **Poor CRM hygiene** — deal records are incomplete or out of date, making forecasting unreliable
- **Long sales cycles with no clear next steps** — deals stall because follow-through after meetings is inconsistent
- **No visibility into pipeline risk** — leaders can't tell which deals are healthy and which are drifting until it's too late
- **Scaling into new markets is slow** — the manual sales process doesn't translate efficiently across geographies or product lines
- **Proposals take too long to produce** — quoting is manual, inconsistent, and disconnected from CRM data

## Who Is NOT a Good Fit

**We say no to:**
- Companies with **< €10M revenue and no commercial structure** — the ROI isn't there and the foundation isn't ready
- **CEO-led marketing & sales** with no dedicated ownership — AI amplifies an existing process; it can't replace one that doesn't exist
- **"Just get us more leads"** mindset — AI-Powered Sales is a system-level engagement, not a lead generation campaign
- Companies with **no CRM commitment** or a history of tool-hopping without strategy — the entire service depends on HubSpot as the operational backbone
- **Purely price-driven buyers** — this work requires strategic commitment and partnership, not the cheapest option

**Why this matters:** Bad-fit customers drain resources, reduce profitability, block scalability, and distract from the work that actually drives strategic growth.
