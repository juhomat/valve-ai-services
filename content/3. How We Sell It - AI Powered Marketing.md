# How We Sell It — AI-Powered Marketing

## Channels

**Inbound — Website**
Our website is the core inbound engine. Service pages, case studies, and the insights blog should speak directly to the pains of the ICP: a small marketing team producing content manually, campaigns that can't be connected to pipeline, CRM data too messy to segment properly, and zero visibility into whether the brand appears when buyers ask AI tools about the category. Decision-makers at €30–100M B2B companies search for answers to these problems before they search for a vendor. Every page and article should make the reader feel understood — and position us as the team that knows how to fix it. SEO should target the specific searches our ICP makes when the pain becomes urgent.

**Thought Leadership**
Beyond the website, we need a visible point of view on AI in B2B marketing — not just "here's what HubSpot can do" but genuine perspective on what it actually takes to make AI work in a complex marketing environment, what the Loop Marketing framework means in practice, and why AEO is becoming as important as SEO. LinkedIn is the primary distribution channel for this — both as a company page and through individual voices from the team. Thought leadership creates the kind of credibility that website content alone can't. This may also mean speaking at events, contributing to industry conversations, and building a reputation as the team that actually deploys AI across a real B2B marketing operation.

**HubSpot Partnership**
HubSpot is a strategic channel, not just a marketplace listing. As a Diamond Solution Partner, we should actively position ourselves within HubSpot's partner ecosystem as the go-to expert for AI-powered marketing implementations. This means building a relationship with HubSpot's partner team, being visible in partner programmes, and making sure HubSpot's own sales and solutions teams know what we do and who to refer. When a HubSpot rep has a customer asking about AI in marketing, we should be the name that comes up. The partner marketplace listing supports this — it captures warm, high-intent prospects already on HubSpot or actively evaluating it — but the deeper value is in the relationship and the positioning within HubSpot's network.

**Existing Client Base — Upsell & Cross-Sell**
The most efficient path to early revenue. We already work with companies that match the ICP perfectly. Clients on Growth Engine retainers — HubSpot, ABM, demand generation — are natural candidates. They have the CRM foundation, they trust us, and the conversation is an extension of work already in progress. The AI-powered marketing conversation starts with "here's what we can do with what you already have" rather than a cold pitch.

**Referrals & Network**
Complex B2B buyers trust peer recommendations over vendor marketing. Happy clients become the most credible sales channel. Structured referral conversations should be built into the delivery process.

**Outbound — Setting It Up for Ourselves**
We should run AI-Powered Marketing on our own operation — not as a marketing stunt, but as a genuine operational choice. This means:
- Configuring our audience segments and buyer intent monitoring in HubSpot to identify ICP-fit accounts showing research or growth signals
- Using the Breeze Prospecting Agent to monitor for buying signals: new Head of Marketing hires, funding rounds, companies expanding into new markets, visible but ad hoc marketing activity
- Running AEO monitoring on ourselves — understanding our own AI search visibility and what competitors are appearing for instead
- Using Content Remix to systematically turn our thought leadership content into multi-channel distribution across LinkedIn, email, and ads
- Setting up outbound sequences that speak to the specific pains of each segment

This serves two purposes: it builds our own pipeline efficiently, and it creates first-hand proof that the service works — which is the most credible thing we can bring into a sales conversation.

## Messaging

**Core message:**
*Your marketing team is producing content manually, sending the same message to everyone, and can't prove which activities are driving pipeline. We build the AI-powered system — on HubSpot — that changes that.*

**Supporting messages by pain point:**

| Pain | Message |
|------|---------|
| Content production is slow and the calendar is always behind | "AI handles content drafting and distribution across every channel. Your team focuses on strategy and quality, not production." |
| Everyone gets the same email and message | "Every segment gets content built for them — intent-based, lifecycle-aware, and consistent in brand voice." |
| Marketing can't prove what's driving pipeline | "Attribution reporting connects every campaign activity — emails, pages, social posts, ads — to pipeline and closed-won revenue." |
| No visibility in AI search engines | "AEO tracks your brand's presence in ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity — and tells you exactly what to create to appear where your buyers are looking." |
| CRM data is too messy to use for targeting | "Data enrichment, quality monitoring, and AI-powered segmentation give you an audience you can actually trust and act on." |
| Competitor activity is tracked manually or not at all | "Competitive monitoring runs automatically — AI search share of voice, CRM competitor mentions, call transcript analysis — and feeds directly into your content strategy." |

**What we are NOT saying:**
- "We implement HubSpot" (too tactical, positions us as a tool vendor)
- "We create content for you" (wrong expectation — we build the system that produces content at scale; the client's team reviews and publishes)
- "AI replaces your marketing team" (wrong audience, wrong message, and wrong)
- "We'll get you leads right away" (this is a compounding system, not a lead generation campaign)

## Sales Motion

The process always starts the same way — with getting the meeting and running discovery. After that, the path splits depending on whether the customer already has HubSpot or not.

---

**Step 0: Booking** *(outbound only)*

When we approach a contact who has never heard of us, the goal is simple: earn a conversation. They don't know what we do, they haven't been looking for us, and they owe us nothing. The outreach has to be relevant enough to be worth their time.

This is where our own AI-powered marketing setup runs. The Breeze Prospecting Agent monitors target accounts for buying signals — a new Head of Marketing hired, a funding round closed, a company visibly expanding into new markets — and surfaces the right moment to reach out. The first message isn't a pitch for our service. It's evidence that we've done our homework, paired with a specific, low-friction ask: a 20-minute conversation.

What good outreach at this step looks like:
- Opens with a relevant signal or observation about their business ("I noticed you've recently expanded into the German market...")
- Connects it briefly to a problem we solve ("Companies scaling into new markets usually hit a wall with how their marketing handles the content and messaging volume...")
- Makes a clear, easy ask ("Would a 20-minute call be worth it?")
- Is short — three to four sentences maximum
- Is sent from a named person, not a company account

The goal of Step 0 is one thing: get the discovery meeting booked.

---

**Step 1: Discovery** *(always)*

Regardless of how the contact came in — outbound, inbound, referral, or existing client — discovery follows the same logic. We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account, contact records are enriched, and any buying signals have been reviewed. We know the company before walking in.

The goal is diagnosis, not pitching. We need to understand:
- Their current marketing setup and where it breaks down
- Team structure and who owns what in marketing today
- CRM situation — what they're on, how well it's working, how clean the data is
- How content is produced and distributed — and how long it takes
- Whether they have any attribution or reporting connecting marketing to pipeline
- What a win would look like for them in the next 6–12 months

The key qualifying question to resolve before the call ends: **do they already have HubSpot?** That determines which path follows.

---

*After discovery, the path splits:*

### Path A — New to HubSpot (or migrating from another platform)

**2. Proposal**
A scoped proposal covering both the HubSpot implementation and the AI-powered marketing layers built on top of it. These are sold together — the CRM and Marketing Hub are the foundation the AI depends on. The proposal maps which of the four marketing functions are in scope and what the full system will look like.

**3. Full implementation**
HubSpot is set up as the commercial and marketing backbone — CRM architecture, Marketing Hub, data model, lifecycle stages — and AI-powered marketing is built on top from the start. Both are delivered as one integrated engagement, not two separate projects. Moves to an ongoing retainer for tuning, iteration, and expansion.

---

### Path B — Already on HubSpot

Customers already on HubSpot can move directly to a full build, or choose to de-risk the commitment with an audit, a pilot, or both. The right entry point depends on their confidence in their current setup and their appetite for a broader engagement.

**2a. Audit** *(optional)*
A structured assessment of their current HubSpot and Marketing Hub setup: data quality, segmentation capability, which AI features are already active, what's working, what's broken, and what needs to be in place before the AI-powered marketing system can be properly built on top. This scopes the work and surfaces quick wins. Recommended when the CRM setup is unclear or Marketing Hub has been used only for basic email sends — we need to know what we're building on before we build.

**2b. Pilot** *(optional)*
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two marketing functions where AI will have the most immediate impact. For example: content pipelines and social distribution, or CRM data quality and segmentation. Recommended when the customer wants to prove value internally before committing to the full system — it creates champions and de-risks the broader decision.

**3. Build AI-powered marketing across the full operation**
Roll out the complete system across all four marketing functions — CRM data analysis, content pipelines, competitive monitoring, and campaign reporting — on top of their existing HubSpot setup. Can follow an audit, a pilot, or come directly after discovery if the customer is ready to commit. Moves to an ongoing retainer for monitoring, tuning, and ongoing development.

## What the First Conversation Looks Like

We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account and buying signals have been reviewed. We know the company before walking in.

The goal of the first conversation is diagnosis, not pitching. The first question to resolve is which path applies — do they have HubSpot already? — as it shapes everything that follows.

**Opening questions to ask:**
- "What does your current marketing setup look like — are you on HubSpot, or something else?"
- "How much of your marketing team's time goes into producing content vs. thinking about strategy?"
- "When you run a campaign, how do you know whether it worked?"
- "How well do your contact segments reflect what you actually know about your audience?"
- "If you search for what you offer in ChatGPT or Perplexity — do you come up?"

**What to listen for:**
- No HubSpot or a disconnected tool stack → Path A, full implementation
- HubSpot in place but messy data or basic email use only → Path B starting with audit
- Content team stretched thin or production bottleneck → Content pipelines is the hook
- No attribution or vague answers on marketing ROI → Campaign reporting and attribution is the hook
- Sending the same message to everyone, no real segmentation → CRM data and AI segmentation is the hook
- Competitive anxiety, no visibility in AI search → AEO and competitive monitoring is the hook

**How to make it concrete:**
Walk through a specific scenario from the service — for example, how Content Remix turns one blog post into LinkedIn posts, a nurture email, a landing page, and ad copy in minutes, or how the AEO tool surfaces share of voice against named competitors across ChatGPT and Perplexity. The goal is to shift the conversation from abstract AI promises to something they can immediately picture in their own marketing operation.

## Pricing Approach

Pricing is not finalised but should reflect the following logic, mapped to the two paths:

**Path A — Full implementation (HubSpot + AI-powered marketing)**
A single project fee covering HubSpot and Marketing Hub setup and AI-powered marketing configuration together, followed by an ongoing retainer. Priced by scope and which of the four marketing functions are covered. Around €10,000–15,000.

**Path B — Audit**
A fixed-fee scoping engagement. Produces a clear assessment of the current HubSpot and Marketing Hub setup and a roadmap for AI-powered marketing. Priced to be easy to approve — the goal is to start the relationship and earn the pilot. Around €3,000–4,000.

**Path B — Pilot**
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two marketing functions. Priced as a low-commitment entry point designed to prove value and build internal buy-in, not to maximise margin on the first engagement. Around €5,000–6,000.

**Path B — Full build**
Once the pilot is complete, a project fee for rolling out the remaining functions, followed by an ongoing retainer. Around €10,000–15,000.

**Ongoing retainer (both paths)**
Monthly engagement covering monitoring, iteration, tuning, and expansion. The system requires active management — segments need updating as the audience evolves, AEO recommendations need acting on, the Evolve stage requires regular review sessions to feed learnings into the next campaign cycle. 10–30 hr/month.

**What to avoid:**
- Pure project pricing with no retainer — the system degrades without ongoing attention. Always try to include an ongoing relationship.
- Pricing by tool or feature — this should be sold as a marketing system, not a HubSpot add-on.
