# What It Is — AI-Powered Service

## Service Definition

AI-Powered Service is a service that introduces AI into every key function of the B2B customer service and customer success operation — from ticket handling and self-service to health scoring, feedback analysis, and service reporting — built on HubSpot's Service Hub as the backbone. The service is designed for growth-oriented mid-market companies (€30–100M) in industrial, deep-tech, and B2B technology sectors that have a customer base worth retaining and a service team that needs to handle more volume without proportionally growing headcount.

We either build the full system from scratch alongside a HubSpot Service Hub implementation, or layer AI-powered capabilities on top of an existing setup. The result is a service operation where AI handles the routine resolution, triage, and monitoring work — so service and customer success teams focus on the relationships, escalations, and strategic conversations that require human judgment.

## What AI Does at Each Service Function

### Ticket Management & Resolution

Ticket management is the operational centre of the service function. Every inbound request — whether it arrives by email, chat, phone, or form — needs to be triaged, categorised, routed to the right person, and resolved as quickly as possible. HubSpot's AI works across the full resolution funnel: attempting autonomous resolution first, then supporting human agents when it can't.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Breeze Customer Agent — Autonomous First-Line Resolution**

The Breeze Customer Agent is HubSpot's autonomous AI support agent. It handles inbound customer inquiries across channels — email, live chat, the customer portal — without requiring a human rep to be involved, drawing on the knowledge base, historical ticket data, and the customer's CRM record to produce accurate, personalised responses.

In practice, the Customer Agent resolves approximately 65% of support conversations without human intervention. It handles high-volume, repeatable Tier 1 requests — product questions, account access, billing queries, how-to questions — and escalates to a human rep when the inquiry is complex, sensitive, or outside what the knowledge base covers.

When it escalates, it doesn't start from scratch. The full conversation context, the customer's CRM history, and the reason for escalation are all passed to the human agent automatically.

The Customer Agent learns over time. When it encounters questions it cannot answer from existing content, it logs these as knowledge gaps — and the Breeze Knowledge Base Agent picks them up and drafts new articles to fill them. Published articles feed back into the Customer Agent's knowledge, creating a closed loop: every unresolved inquiry eventually improves the system's ability to resolve similar inquiries in the future.

The Customer Agent works across:
- Email
- Live chat and website chat
- Customer portal
- SMS and WhatsApp (where configured)

Pricing is outcome-based at €0.50 per resolved conversation. Available on Service Hub Professional and Enterprise.

**AI Ticket Triage, Categorisation & Routing**

When tickets arrive that the Customer Agent cannot fully resolve — or where a human rep needs to be involved from the start — HubSpot's AI takes over the triage work automatically.

- **Automatic categorisation** — analyses the content of the incoming message and assigns a category (billing, product issue, account access, general enquiry, etc.) without manual tagging
- **Sentiment detection** — identifies whether the customer's message is positive, negative, or neutral, and flags high-urgency or high-frustration tickets for immediate attention
- **Language detection** — automatically detects the language of the incoming inquiry and routes accordingly
- **Intelligent routing** — based on content analysis, sentiment, category, and configured rules, routes each ticket to the right team or agent
- **Automatic ticket naming** — generates a descriptive ticket name from the message content so reps understand the issue before opening it

**AI-Suggested Replies — Real-Time Rep Assistance**

As support reps work on tickets, HubSpot's AI generates suggested responses in real time. Suggestions are drawn from the knowledge base, historical resolved tickets, and the customer's CRM data. The rep reviews, edits if needed, and sends — or discards and writes their own.

This significantly reduces the time reps spend composing replies from scratch, while keeping a human in the loop on every outbound response.

**Ticket Summarisation — Instant Context for Handoffs and Escalations**

Any ticket thread can be summarised with a single click. The AI scans the full conversation history and produces a concise paragraph covering the key issue, what's been tried, and what the customer is asking for. This is particularly valuable for:
- Agent handoffs — a new rep gets full context in seconds
- Escalations — a senior rep or manager can understand the situation without reading the full thread
- End-of-ticket documentation — a clean summary attached to the record

**SLA Management — Breach Prevention and Monitoring**

Service Hub allows SLA policies to be configured for time to first response and time to close. Once set, the system:
- Shows a real-time countdown on every open ticket
- Sends automated alerts before an SLA breach occurs — at a configurable threshold (e.g., alert 4 hours before deadline)
- Tracks SLA performance across the team with dashboard reporting

SLA monitoring is not AI in the predictive sense, but it's a critical automated layer that keeps service quality consistent without relying on reps to watch the clock.

**Conversation Intelligence — AI Analysis of Support Calls**

For service teams handling support by phone, Conversation Intelligence automatically records, transcribes, and analyses calls. It extracts:
- Call summaries
- Action items and follow-ups
- Sentiment signals across the conversation
- Competitor mentions or recurring themes
- Coaching opportunities for team leads

Tracked terms can be configured — so if specific product names, common complaints, or competitor references keep appearing in calls, the system surfaces them automatically. Available on Service Hub Professional and Enterprise.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Help desk configuration** — building the ticket pipeline (stages, properties, views), connecting inbound channels (email, chat, portal), and ensuring every incoming request lands in the right place
- **Breeze Customer Agent setup** — connecting knowledge sources (knowledge base, website, documentation), configuring escalation rules and handoff conditions, setting the tone and response parameters, and running the first batch of test conversations before going live
- **AI triage and routing rules** — configuring categorisation logic, sentiment-based priority rules, and team routing so every ticket lands with the right person automatically
- **Suggested replies activation** — connecting the reply recommendation engine to the knowledge base and enabling it for all reps in the help desk
- **SLA policy configuration** — defining working hours, SLA targets for each ticket type, alert thresholds, and breach notification workflows
- **Conversation Intelligence setup** — enabling call recording, configuring keyword tracking for the client's most important themes, and building the first coaching and quality review process
- **Omnichannel connection** — connecting email, chat, and where relevant SMS and WhatsApp to the unified help desk so all conversations are tracked in one place

#### What the Service Team Still Does

- Reviewing and approving Breeze Customer Agent responses for edge cases before enabling autonomous mode
- Handling escalated tickets that require human judgment, authority, or relationship context
- Reviewing suggested replies and applying editorial judgment before sending

- Monitoring call transcripts for coaching opportunities and acting on them

### Customer Self-Service

Self-service is the most scalable layer of the service operation. Every ticket that a customer resolves themselves — by finding the answer in a knowledge base article, using the customer portal, or getting an immediate response from the Customer Agent on the website — is a ticket that never enters the support queue. HubSpot's AI helps build and maintain the self-service layer, and keeps it improving over time.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Breeze Knowledge Base Agent — AI-Generated Documentation**

The Knowledge Base Agent works in tandem with the Breeze Customer Agent to identify and fill gaps in the existing documentation. When the Customer Agent encounters questions it cannot answer from existing content, those gaps are logged. The Knowledge Base Agent analyses these gaps alongside historical ticket patterns and successful resolution strategies, then drafts new knowledge base articles ready for team review.

The workflow is:
1. Customer Agent encounters an unanswerable question — logs as a gap
2. Knowledge Base Agent analyses the gap pattern alongside historical tickets
3. AI drafts a new article based on how reps have successfully resolved similar questions
4. Team reviews and publishes
5. Published article feeds back into the Customer Agent's knowledge for future queries

This means the knowledge base grows and improves based on what customers are actually asking — not based on what the team assumes they'll ask. Available on Service Hub Professional and Enterprise.

**Knowledge Base — The Foundation of Self-Service**

The knowledge base is where customers find answers without contacting support. HubSpot's knowledge base is built directly on the CRM — so articles can reference product data, and the Customer Agent can draw on them in real time.

Articles are organised by category, searchable, and accessible from the customer portal, the website chat widget, and any support email. Teams can see which articles are most viewed, which searches return no results, and which articles have the lowest satisfaction ratings — all of which feed back into the improvement cycle.

**Customer Portal — The Self-Service Hub for Existing Customers**

The customer portal is a branded, login-protected space where existing customers can:
- Submit new support tickets without sending an email
- Track the status of open tickets in real time
- View resolved ticket history
- Access the knowledge base
- Upload supporting files or documentation

It reduces the back-and-forth of email-based support and gives customers visibility into their own requests without having to chase the support team. Access control is configurable — customers can be limited to viewing only their own tickets or expanded to see all tickets from their company.

Available on Service Hub Professional and Enterprise.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Knowledge base setup** — structuring the category architecture, writing the first set of foundational articles from existing documentation and the most common support tickets, and configuring the knowledge base's appearance and access settings
- **Knowledge Base Agent activation** — enabling the agent and connecting it to the help desk so gaps are automatically logged and drafted articles enter a review queue
- **Initial gap analysis** — running the first gap analysis against the existing ticket history to identify the highest-impact articles to create first
- **Customer portal setup** — configuring the portal with the client's branding, setting access control rules, defining which ticket properties customers can see, and connecting it to the knowledge base
- **Chat widget integration** — embedding the knowledge base search and Customer Agent access point on the client's website so visitors can self-serve before a ticket is ever created

#### What the Service Team Still Does

- Reviewing and approving AI-drafted knowledge base articles before publication
- Providing subject matter expertise for articles on complex technical topics
- Making editorial decisions on tone and depth of knowledge base content
- Monitoring knowledge base usage data and identifying which articles need updating
- Deciding which customer portal features to expose to which customer segments

### Customer Success & Retention

Customer success is distinct from customer support. Support is reactive — a customer has a problem and needs help. Customer success is proactive — identifying which customers are healthy, which are at risk, and intervening before a problem becomes a churn event or a missed expansion opportunity. HubSpot's Customer Success Workspace and health scoring tools bring AI into this proactive layer.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Customer Health Score — Continuous Account Health Monitoring**

Customer health scores aggregate signals from across the CRM into a single score that reflects how well each account is doing — and whether it's at risk. Rather than a manual quarterly review, health scores update continuously as new data comes in.

Signals that feed into a health score typically include:
- Product usage data (if connected)
- Support ticket volume and resolution speed
- Survey scores (NPS, CSAT)
- Email engagement levels
- Meeting participation
- Renewal and contract status
- Open deal or expansion activity

Positive signals increase the score; risk indicators decrease it. The result is a continuous, prioritised view of the customer base — so CSMs know which accounts need attention without having to manually review every record.

Basic health scores are available at Service Hub Professional. Custom health score algorithms and predictive analytics are Enterprise-only.

**Breeze Customer Health Agent — AI-Powered Account Intelligence (Enterprise)**

The Customer Health Agent is a Breeze AI agent specifically built for customer success managers. It assesses account health and recommends specific next actions to reduce churn risk or unlock expansion opportunities.

When a CSM opens an account, the Health Agent draws from:
- Call notes and transcripts
- Meeting history
- Email conversations
- Deal and opportunity data
- Support ticket history
- Health score trends

And produces:
- An assessment of current account health and risk level
- Tailored talking points for the next customer conversation
- Discovery questions for the CSM to ask
- Outreach recommendations
- Draft email templates for follow-up or renewal conversations

Currently in beta on Enterprise tier.

**Customer Success Workspace — The CSM's Operating Hub**

The Customer Success Workspace is a purpose-built view inside HubSpot that brings together everything a CSM needs to manage their book of business:

- Health score overview across all accounts
- Real-time alerts when a health score drops below a configured threshold
- Customer schedules, tasks, and upcoming meetings
- NPS, CSAT, and CES scores per account
- Guided playbooks for common scenarios (renewal, expansion, at-risk intervention)
- Key reports and metrics visible without leaving the workspace

For service teams managing post-sale relationships, this replaces the need to pull together data from multiple reports or tools before each customer interaction.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Customer Success Workspace configuration** — setting up the workspace, configuring the book-of-business view, and building the alert logic that notifies CSMs when health scores change
- **Health score design** — working with the client to define which signals matter for their customer base, how to weight them, and what thresholds define healthy, at-risk, and critical accounts
- **Health score build** — configuring the health score model in HubSpot, connecting the relevant data sources, and validating the initial scores against known good-fit and at-risk accounts
- **Proactive playbooks** — building the standard CSM plays in HubSpot: at-risk intervention, renewal conversation, expansion discovery, onboarding check-in
- **Breeze Customer Health Agent setup** — where on Enterprise, configuring the agent with the client's account context, product information, and success criteria (beta)
- **Alert and escalation workflows** — building workflows that trigger when accounts hit risk thresholds: CSM alert, management notification, or enrolment in a re-engagement sequence

#### What the Customer Success Team Still Does

- Building and maintaining relationships with customers — the health score informs but does not replace the conversation
- Making the judgment call on when and how to intervene with at-risk accounts
- Running the renewal and expansion conversations
- Interpreting health score trends and deciding on strategic responses
- Providing feedback on which signals the health score should weight more or less over time

### Voice of the Customer & Feedback

Feedback data is one of the most underused assets in most service operations. NPS and CSAT surveys are sent, scores are collected, and very little happens with the open-text responses. HubSpot's AI changes this by analysing feedback at scale — surfacing themes, detecting sentiment, and connecting survey outcomes back to the CRM records that triggered them.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**NPS, CSAT & CES Surveys — Structured Feedback Collection**

HubSpot supports all three standard customer feedback survey types:

- **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — measures overall likelihood to recommend; useful for tracking relationship health over time
- **CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score)** — measures satisfaction with a specific interaction or resolution; used post-ticket or post-meeting
- **CES (Customer Effort Score)** — measures how easy it was for the customer to get their issue resolved; a strong predictor of churn

Surveys are sent automatically based on triggers — ticket closed, meeting completed, X days post-onboarding — and responses flow directly back into the customer's CRM record.

**AI Sentiment Analysis — Understanding What Customers Are Actually Saying**

Beyond the numerical score, customers write open-text feedback. AI automatically analyses these responses to:
- Detect sentiment (positive, negative, neutral) in written feedback
- Identify recurring themes across responses — what topics keep coming up?
- Flag high-urgency or high-frustration responses for immediate follow-up
- Generate summaries of feedback across a time period or customer segment

This means the team gets insight from every response — not just the ones someone happened to read manually.

**Feedback-to-CRM Automation**

Survey responses flow automatically into the customer's contact and company record. This means:
- Detractor NPS scores can trigger an immediate CSM alert or re-engagement workflow
- Low CSAT scores on a ticket can flag the ticket for quality review
- Promoter responses can trigger a referral or case study conversation request

Feedback stops being a reporting metric and becomes an operational signal.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Survey configuration** — building NPS, CSAT, and CES surveys in HubSpot, configuring the trigger logic (which events send which survey to which contacts), and setting up delivery via email or in-product
- **Feedback-to-CRM property mapping** — ensuring survey responses, scores, and sentiment classifications flow into the right CRM properties on the contact and company record
- **Automated follow-up workflows** — building workflows that act on survey outcomes: detractor follow-up sequence, promoter referral ask, low CSAT quality review flag, CSM alert on health score drop
- **Feedback reporting dashboard** — building the reports that show NPS trends over time, CSAT by ticket type or agent, CES by interaction, and the most common feedback themes

#### What the Service Team Still Does

- Deciding which survey types to use and when to send them
- Reviewing AI-summarised feedback themes and drawing strategic conclusions
- Following up personally with detractors and at-risk customers
- Interpreting NPS trends and deciding how to respond at an operational level
- Deciding whether and how to share feedback results with product or sales teams

### Reporting & Insights

Service reporting exists to answer two questions: is the team performing well, and are customers getting what they need? HubSpot's Service Hub reporting connects operational metrics (response times, SLA performance, agent productivity) with customer outcome metrics (health scores, feedback trends, resolution rates) — and AI helps surface the insights that would otherwise require hours of manual report analysis.

#### What HubSpot's AI Does

**Service Analytics — Out-of-the-Box Performance Reporting**

Service Hub includes a set of pre-built reports covering the core operational metrics of a support team:

- Average first response time and time to close
- Ticket volume by channel, category, and time period
- SLA adherence rate — tickets meeting vs. breaching targets
- Agent productivity and utilisation
- Customer feedback scores (NPS, CSAT, CES) over time
- Breeze Customer Agent resolution rate — how many conversations the AI resolved vs. escalated

These reports are available without custom configuration and provide an immediate baseline view of service performance.

**AI-Generated Report Insights — Plain-Language Summaries**

For any custom report built in HubSpot, Breeze generates an AI-powered summary of what the report shows — key takeaways, notable patterns, and suggested areas to investigate. Instead of interpreting charts manually, the team gets a plain-language interpretation alongside the data. The same capability applies to campaign summaries and dashboard overviews.

**Breeze Data Agent — Custom Service Questions at Scale**

The Breeze Data Agent answers custom questions across the full CRM — drawing from ticket history, contact records, conversation logs, and feedback data. For service reporting, this means being able to ask:

- "Which ticket categories have the highest re-open rate?"
- "Which customers have submitted more than three tickets in the last 90 days and have an NPS below 6?"
- "Which support reps have the fastest average time to close across high-priority tickets?"

Results flow back into lists and workflows — so insights can trigger automatic actions rather than sitting in a dashboard.

**Health Score Trend Reporting**

Customer health score data is reportable alongside all other CRM data. Teams can build reports showing:
- Health score distribution across the customer base at a point in time
- Health score trends over time per account
- Correlation between health score and renewal or churn outcomes
- Which signals most reliably predict at-risk status

Over time, this data allows the health score model to be refined based on what actually predicted churn vs. what turned out to be a false alarm.

#### What We Set Up for the Customer

- **Service analytics dashboard** — building the primary operational dashboard combining ticket performance, SLA metrics, agent productivity, and Customer Agent resolution rates
- **Feedback and health reporting** — building reports connecting NPS, CSAT, and health score trends over time, segmented by account tier, CSM, and product area
- **Breeze Data Agent service queries** — defining the key questions the client's service and success leaders need to answer regularly and configuring Data Agent queries that surface those insights automatically
- **Executive reporting view** — building a simplified high-level dashboard for leadership showing the metrics that matter for service quality and customer retention
- **Health score correlation analysis** — building the first reports that connect health score movements to actual renewal and churn outcomes, so the model can be validated and refined

#### What the Service Team Still Does

- Interpreting reports and drawing operational and strategic conclusions
- Deciding how to respond to performance trends — what needs to change, and who owns the change
- Presenting results to leadership and making the case for resource or process adjustments
- Refining health score weighting based on what correlation data reveals over time


## What This Is Not

- **Not a contact centre platform** — Service Hub is a CRM-native service tool built for B2B mid-market teams. It is not a large-scale contact centre or call routing platform. Companies with high-volume inbound phone support at enterprise scale may need dedicated contact centre software alongside or instead.
- **Not a replacement for the service team** — AI handles autonomous resolution for repeatable Tier 1 requests, assists reps with triage and suggested replies, and monitors account health. The service and customer success team still owns the relationship, the escalation, the renewal conversation, and every interaction that requires human judgment or authority.
- **Not a one-off implementation** — the Breeze Customer Agent improves as the knowledge base grows. Health scores need tuning as more renewal and churn data accumulates. Feedback themes need acting on. The system requires ongoing maintenance, and a retainer is what keeps it performing and improving.
- **Not a product analytics or customer data platform** — HubSpot can connect to product usage data where an integration exists, but it is not a dedicated product analytics tool. Health scores that depend on deep product usage signals may require an integration with a purpose-built product analytics platform.
- **Not purely a HubSpot implementation** — HubSpot Service Hub is the backbone, but the service is the commercial system built on top of it: the knowledge architecture, the triage logic, the health score model, the feedback loops, and the playbooks that make the whole operation coherent.
