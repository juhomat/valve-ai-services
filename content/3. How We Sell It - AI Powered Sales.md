# How We Sell It — AI-Powered Sales

## Channels

**Inbound — Website**
Our website is the core inbound engine. Service pages, case studies, and the insights blog should speak directly to the pains of the ICP: reps drowning in admin, deals stalling after meetings, CRM that nobody trusts, pipeline reviews that feel like guesswork. Decision-makers at €30–100M B2B companies search for answers to these problems before they search for a vendor. Every page and article should make the reader feel understood — and position us as the team that knows how to fix it. SEO should target the specific searches our ICP makes when the pain becomes urgent.

**Thought Leadership**
Beyond the website, we need a visible point of view on AI in B2B sales — not just "here's what HubSpot can do" but genuine perspective on where AI fits, where it doesn't, and what it takes to make it work in complex sales environments.LinkedIn is the primary distribution channel for this — both as a company page and through individual voices from the team. Thought leadership creates the kind of credibility that website content alone can't. Possibly also this means speaking at events, contributing to industry conversations, and building a reputation as the team that actually knows how to deploy AI across a real B2B sales process. 

**HubSpot Partnership**
HubSpot is a strategic channel, not just a marketplace listing. As a Diamond Solution Partner, we should actively position ourselves within HubSpot's partner ecosystem as the go-to expert for AI-powered sales implementations. This means building a relationship with HubSpot's partner team, being visible in partner programmes, and making sure HubSpot's own sales and solutions teams know what we do and who to refer. When a HubSpot rep has a customer asking about AI in sales, we should be the name that comes up. The partner marketplace listing supports this — it captures warm, high-intent prospects already on HubSpot or actively evaluating it — but the deeper value is in the relationship and the positioning within HubSpot's network.

**Existing Client Base — Upsell & Cross-Sell**
The most efficient path to early revenue. We already work with companies that match the ICP perfectly. Clients on Growth Engine retainers (HubSpot, ABM, demand gen) are natural candidates — they have the CRM foundation and the commercial ambition. The conversation is an extension of work already trusted, not a cold sale.

**Referrals & Network**
Complex B2B buyers trust peer recommendations over vendor marketing. Happy clients become the most credible sales channel. Structured referral conversations should be built into the delivery process.

**Outbound — Setting It Up for Ourselves**
We should run its own AI-powered sales setup on its own target account list — not as a marketing stunt, but as a genuine operational choice. This means:
- Building a selling profile and buying profile for our own ICP in HubSpot
- Configuring the Breeze Prospecting Agent to monitor the target account list for buying signals (funding rounds, leadership changes, new hires in sales/marketing)
- Connecting a data provider (Apollo or similar) to source and enrich contacts at ICP-fit accounts
- Setting up personalised outreach sequences that speak to the specific pains of each segment (industrial tech, professional services, climate/energy tech)
- Using the Company Research Agent to brief reps before outbound calls
- Running Smart Deal Progression to keep outbound deals moving after every conversation

This serves two purposes: it builds our own pipeline efficiently, and it creates authentic, first-hand proof that the service works — which is the most credible thing we can bring into a sales conversation.

## Sales Motion

The process always starts the same way — with getting the meeting and running discovery. After that, the path splits depending on whether the customer already has HubSpot or not.

---

**Step 0: Booking** *(outbound only)*

When we approach a contact who has never heard of us, the goal is simple: earn a conversation. They don't know what we do, they haven't been looking for us, and they owe us nothing. The outreach has to be relevant enough to be worth their time.

This is where our own AI-powered setup runs. The Breeze Prospecting Agent monitors the target account for buying signals — a new Head of Sales hired, a funding round closed, a competitor technology adopted — and drafts personalised outreach anchored to something that actually happened at their company. The first message isn't a pitch for our service. It's evidence that we've done our homework, paired with a specific, low-friction ask: a 20-minute conversation.

What good outreach at this step looks like:
- Opens with a relevant signal or observation about their business ("I noticed you're expanding into the German market...")
- Connects it briefly to a problem we solve ("Companies scaling into new markets usually hit a wall with how their sales process handles the volume...")
- Makes a clear, easy ask ("Would a 20-minute call be worth it?")
- Is short — three to four sentences maximum
- Is sent from a named person, not a company account

The goal of Step 0 is one thing: get the discovery meeting booked.

---

**Step 1: Discovery** *(always)*

Regardless of how the contact came in — outbound, inbound, referral, or existing client — discovery follows the same logic. We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account, contact records are enriched, and buying signals have been reviewed. We know the company before walking in.

The goal is diagnosis, not pitching. We need to understand:
- Their current sales process and where it breaks down
- Team structure and how selling happens today
- CRM situation — what they're on, how well it's working, how much the team actually uses it
- Where time is being lost and where deals are stalling
- What a win would look like for them in the next 6–12 months

The key qualifying question to resolve before the call ends: **do they already have HubSpot?** That determines which path follows.

---

*After discovery, the path splits:*

### Path A — New to HubSpot (or migrating from another CRM)

**2. Proposal**
A scoped proposal covering both the HubSpot implementation and the AI-powered sales layers built on top of it. These are sold together — the CRM is the foundation the AI depends on. The proposal maps which of the five sales stages are in scope and what the full system will look like.

**3. Full implementation**
HubSpot is set up as the commercial backbone — CRM architecture, pipeline, lifecycle stages, data model — and AI-powered sales is built on top from the start. Both are delivered as one integrated engagement, not two separate projects. Moves to an ongoing retainer for tuning, iteration, and expansion.

---

### Path B — Already on HubSpot

Customers already on HubSpot can move directly to a full build, or choose to de-risk the commitment with an audit, a pilot, or both. The right entry point depends on their confidence in their current setup and their appetite for a broader engagement.

**2a. Audit** *(optional)*
A structured assessment of their current HubSpot setup: data quality, pipeline configuration, what's working, what's broken, and what needs to be in place before AI can be layered on top. This scopes the work and surfaces quick wins. Recommended when the CRM setup is unclear or hasn't been actively maintained — we need to know what we're building on before we build.

**2b. Pilot** *(optional)*
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two sales stages where AI will have the most immediate impact. For example: prospecting and research, or meeting prep and closing. Recommended when the customer wants to prove value internally before committing to the full system — it creates champions and de-risks the broader decision.

**3. Build AI-powered sales across the full process**
Roll out the complete system across all five stages on top of their existing HubSpot setup. Can follow an audit, a pilot, or come directly after discovery if the customer is ready to commit. Moves to an ongoing retainer for monitoring, tuning, and ongoing development.

## What the First Conversation Looks Like

We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account and buying signals have been reviewed. We know the company before walking in.

The goal of the first conversation is diagnosis, not pitching. The first question to resolve is which path applies — do they have HubSpot already? — as it shapes everything that follows.

**Opening questions to ask:**
- "What does your current CRM setup look like — are you on HubSpot, or something else?"
- "How much time do your reps spend on research, CRM updates, and writing emails — vs. actually talking to prospects?"
- "Where do deals most commonly stall in your pipeline?"
- "How confident are you in your pipeline forecast right now?"
- "If you could fix one thing about how your sales process runs today, what would it be?"

**What to listen for:**
- No HubSpot or weak CRM → Path A, full implementation
- HubSpot in place but underused or messy → Path B starting with audit
- Frustration with admin and CRM compliance → Prospecting and Smart Deal Progression are the hook
- Unreliable forecast or pipeline visibility → Lead scoring and AI forecasting is the hook
- Slow quoting or proposals → CPQ is the hook
- Deals stalling post-meeting → Meeting prep and closing are the hook

**How to make it concrete:**
Walk through a specific scenario from the service — e.g. how the Closing Agent works, or what a Prospect Intelligence Brief looks like — to shift the conversation from abstract AI promises to something tangible they can picture in their own sales process.

## Pricing Approach

Pricing is not finalised but should reflect the following logic, mapped to the two paths:

**Path A — Full implementation (HubSpot + AI-powered sales)**
A single project fee covering HubSpot setup and AI-powered sales configuration together, followed by an ongoing retainer. Priced by scope and number of sales stages covered. Around 10 000 - 15 000 €

**Path B — Audit**
A fixed-fee scoping engagement. Produces a clear assessment of the current HubSpot setup and a roadmap for AI-powered sales. Priced to be easy to approve — the goal is to start the relationship and earn the pilot. Around 3000 - 4000 €.

**Path B — Pilot**
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two sales stages. Priced as a low-commitment entry point designed to prove value and build internal buy-in, not to maximise margin on the first engagement. 5000 € - 6000 €

**Path B — Full build**
Once the pilot is complete, a project fee for rolling out the remaining stages, followed by an ongoing retainer. Around 10 000 - 15 000 €

**Ongoing retainer (both paths)**
Monthly engagement covering monitoring, iteration, tuning, and expansion. The system requires active management — signals need reviewing, scoring models need updating, new templates need building as the product and market evolve. 10 - 30 hr / month.

**What to avoid:**
- Pure project pricing with no retainer — the system degrades without ongoing attention. Try to always invclude ongoing relationship.
- Pricing by tool or feature — this should be sold as a commercial system, not a HubSpot add-on.
