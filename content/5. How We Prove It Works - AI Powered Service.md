# How We Prove It Works — AI-Powered Service

## Key Metrics

Proof needs to be structured at two levels: operational metrics that show the system is running well, and outcome metrics that connect the service system to business results. Operational metrics are visible within weeks. Outcome metrics — retention, churn rate, expansion — take longer to build but are what commercial leaders ultimately care about.

### Operational metrics — the system is working

**Ticket management**
- Breeze Customer Agent resolution rate: % of conversations resolved autonomously without human intervention
- Average time to first response: before vs. after engagement baseline
- Average time to ticket close: before vs. after
- SLA adherence rate: % of tickets meeting first response and close time targets
- Ticket re-open rate: % of resolved tickets that come back — a signal of resolution quality
- AI suggested reply adoption: % of rep replies that start from a suggestion vs. written from scratch

**Self-service**
- Knowledge base article count growth over time
- Knowledge base search-to-resolution rate: % of searches that end without a ticket being submitted
- Knowledge Base Agent gap closure rate: gaps identified vs. articles published
- Customer portal adoption: % of customers submitting tickets via portal vs. email
- Customer Agent deflection rate: tickets prevented because the customer found the answer through self-service

**Customer success**
- Health score coverage: % of active customer accounts with a configured health score
- Alert response time: how quickly CSMs act on health score drop alerts
- At-risk account intervention rate: % of accounts flagged as at-risk that received a proactive outreach

**Feedback**
- Survey response rate: % of triggered surveys that receive a response
- NPS, CSAT, and CES scores per ticket type, channel, and agent — and the trend over time
- Detractor follow-up rate: % of detractor NPS responses that receive a personal outreach within 48 hours

### Outcome metrics — the system is driving results

**Retention**
- Customer retention rate: % of accounts renewed vs. churned over a period, before vs. after engagement
- Early churn identification rate: % of churned accounts that were flagged as at-risk by the health score before the renewal — a measure of the health score's predictive accuracy
- Time between health score alert and intervention: how proactively the team is acting on signals

**Support economics**
- Cost per resolved ticket: total support cost divided by tickets closed, before vs. after the Customer Agent
- Support team capacity freed: estimated hours saved per week from Customer Agent autonomous resolution and AI-assisted triage
- Tickets handled per rep: volume per person before vs. after, showing scale without headcount growth

**Revenue connection**
- Renewal rate: accounts that renewed vs. churned in the period
- Expansion revenue from proactively managed accounts: deals where a CSM-initiated conversation (triggered by a health score alert) led to an upsell or expansion
- NPS correlation with renewal: whether higher NPS scores predict renewal — validating the feedback loop as a leading indicator

## Feedback Loop — Pilots to Service Design

Every engagement generates learnings that should feed back into how the service is designed and delivered. The goal is structured capture, not ad-hoc reflection.

**What to capture from every engagement:**
- Which of the five service functions had the most immediate visible impact — where did the team notice the difference first?
- How did the Breeze Customer Agent perform in the first 90 days? What categories did it struggle with, and why?
- Which knowledge base gaps appeared most frequently in the first month — and how quickly were they filled?
- How accurately did the health score predict at-risk accounts in the first renewal cycle?
- Which health score signals turned out to be reliable predictors, and which were noise?
- What did the service and customer success team adopt readily vs. what required more coaching?
- What product usage data gaps made health scoring harder, and how were they bridged?
- What questions or objections came up during the engagement that weren't anticipated in the sales process?

**How learnings get used:**
- Process adjustments: if the Customer Agent consistently struggles with a category type in a specific industry, the setup process for that industry gets updated
- Scope adjustments: if health scoring delivers the fastest visible results, it becomes the recommended pilot entry point
- Messaging adjustments: if a specific proof point resonates strongly in sales conversations, it gets incorporated into the standard narrative
- Health score model refinement: if certain signals consistently over- or under-predict churn, the model weighting is adjusted as the default recommendation
- Service additions: if a gap keeps appearing — a tool HubSpot doesn't cover, a client need that keeps recurring — it becomes a candidate for adding to the service stack

## How We Talk About Results Externally

**Lead with retention, not ticket volume**
The most commercially resonant proof point for a service leader is retention: more customers renewed, fewer churned, and some expanded. Ticket volume reduction is an operational win; retention improvement is a revenue outcome. The conversation that lands with a CFO or CRO is about the cost of churn vs. the cost of the system — not about how many tickets were closed per hour.

**Make the economics of the Customer Agent concrete**
The Breeze Customer Agent's outcome-based pricing (€0.50 per resolved conversation) makes the ROI calculation unusually transparent. A team handling 500 tickets per month where the agent resolves 65% saves approximately 325 tickets of rep time per month at a cost of €162 in credits. The cost of 325 rep-handled tickets — including salary, overhead, and the slower resolution time — is almost always an order of magnitude higher. This comparison should be part of every external conversation.

**Use the health score as the early warning story**
The most distinctive claim of the service is that the system identifies churn risk before the customer signals it explicitly. The health score story is powerful because it's concrete: a customer whose score drops from 74 to 31 over six weeks gets a CSM alert, a conversation happens, the problem is identified, and the renewal goes ahead. Without the health score, that customer is a surprise churn. The before-and-after is vivid, and it's a narrative that service leaders immediately recognise as something they wish they had.

**Show before and after at the operational level too**
Alongside retention outcomes, operational improvements — response time halved, SLA breaches eliminated, knowledge base articles tripled — are credible proof that the system is working, even before a full renewal cycle has passed. These metrics are visible within the first 90 days and give clients and prospects something concrete to point to early.

**Honest about what's still being learned**
This is a new service built on AI capabilities that are evolving fast. The right framing: we know the framework, we know the platform, we are actively learning what works best for which types of service teams and customer bases — and those learnings feed directly back into every engagement.
