# Who Buys It — AI-Powered Service

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Growth-Oriented Mid-Market — €30–100M revenue**

Industrial, deep-tech, or B2B technology companies with:

- **50–500 employees**
- **A customer base worth retaining** — recurring revenue, renewals, or multi-year contracts where churn and expansion materially affect the business
- **A service or customer success function already in place** — at least one person responsible for support or customer success with real mandate
- **Complex products or services** — where customers need ongoing help, onboarding, or guidance, not just a product they can use without support
- **International operations or growth** — serving customers across multiple markets, languages, or time zones
- **HubSpot already in place** OR actively migrating from a legacy helpdesk or disconnected tool stack

**Why this profile is the right fit:**
- Large enough to have a real customer base where retention loss is commercially significant
- Small enough that the service team is stretched — they're handling volume that requires AI to scale without headcount
- Complex enough offerings that customer success requires active management, not just reactive support
- High potential impact from structured triage, self-service, health scoring, and proactive retention work

**Example customer types:**
- B2B SaaS companies with subscription models where churn directly hits NRR
- Industrial tech companies with long-term service contracts and technical support requirements
- Professional services firms managing ongoing client relationships post-delivery
- Climate and energy tech scale-ups onboarding customers onto complex products in new markets

**Named examples from our client base:** Stora Enso, Virta, Abax, Katana

**Two types of buyer — same ICP, different starting point:**
- **Already on HubSpot** — Service Hub is in place but being used as a basic shared inbox. Tickets are managed manually, there's no AI layer, no knowledge base worth speaking of, and no health scoring. The engagement builds the service system on top of what exists.
- **New to HubSpot or migrating** — currently on a legacy helpdesk (Zendesk, Freshdesk, Jira Service Management) or managing support through email and spreadsheets. The platform migration and the AI-powered service build happen together as one integrated engagement.

The sales motion differs between the two (see How We Sell It), but the ICP profile is the same.

## The Pain They Feel

These companies have a service team and a customer base — but the operation is manual, reactive, and disconnected from the rest of the business. Common pain points that make them ready for AI-Powered Service:

- **Support volume is growing faster than the team** — every new customer adds tickets. The team is hiring to keep up, but the economics don't work at scale. Repetitive, low-complexity tickets are taking the same time as complex ones.
- **Too much tribal knowledge, not enough documentation** — experienced reps know the answers, but that knowledge isn't written down. Onboarding new reps takes months. Customers can't self-serve because there's no knowledge base worth using.
- **No visibility into which customers are at risk** — churn is discovered on the renewal call, not six months before it. There's no early warning system. CSMs are reacting to problems rather than preventing them.
- **Customer success is reactive, not proactive** — CSMs spend their time firefighting open tickets rather than building relationships. There's no structured way to identify which accounts need attention before they become a problem.
- **Service and sales are disconnected** — support reps don't have visibility into what was promised during the sale. Sales reps don't know which of their accounts are struggling with support. Everyone is working from incomplete information.
- **No consistent feedback loop** — NPS surveys are sent occasionally, results sit in a spreadsheet, and almost nothing changes as a result. Open-text feedback is never analysed at scale.
- **Current CRM or helpdesk isn't working** — they're on a tool that's disconnected from the CRM, so every support interaction lacks context. Reps have to switch between systems to find customer history. *(Path A buyers)*
- **Onboarding new customers is inconsistent** — there's no structured onboarding process, so customers who get a good rep have a good experience and those who don't, don't.
- **Reporting is manual and limited** — service leaders don't have a clear view of ticket volume, resolution time, or SLA performance without pulling data manually. There's no connection between service metrics and retention outcomes.

## Who Is NOT a Good Fit

**We say no to:**
- Companies with **high-volume consumer support** — Service Hub is designed for B2B mid-market teams, not large-scale contact centres handling thousands of inbound calls per day from consumers. The economics and the tooling are different.
- **Transactional or one-time purchase businesses** — if there are no ongoing customer relationships to manage, there's no customer success function to build. AI amplifies an existing retention model; it can't create one where none exists.
- **Service teams that don't exist** — if there's no one inside the company owning service or customer success with real mandate, there's no one to operate the system we build. This is the same constraint as in marketing and sales.
- Companies **committed to Zendesk, Freshdesk, or Salesforce Service Cloud** with no intention of migrating — the service is built on HubSpot as the backbone. If there's no appetite to consolidate on HubSpot, the engagement doesn't hold together.
- **Pure project businesses** — companies that deliver a project and have no ongoing relationship with the client post-delivery have no recurring customer base to retain or manage. There's nothing for customer success to operate on.
- **Purely price-driven buyers** — designing a service architecture, building a health score model, and configuring an AI-powered knowledge base requires strategic commitment and partnership, not the cheapest option.

**Why this matters:** Bad-fit customers drain resources, reduce profitability, block scalability, and distract from the work that actually drives strategic growth.
