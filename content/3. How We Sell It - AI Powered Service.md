# How We Sell It — AI-Powered Service

## Channels

**Inbound — Website**
Our website is the core inbound engine. Service pages, case studies, and the insights blog should speak directly to the pains of the ICP: a support team drowning in repetitive tickets, customer churn discovered on the renewal call, knowledge locked in experienced reps' heads, and no clear view of which accounts are healthy and which are at risk. Decision-makers at €30–100M B2B companies — heads of customer success, service managers, COOs — search for answers to these problems before they search for a vendor. Every page and article should make the reader feel understood and position us as the team that knows how to fix it.

**Thought Leadership**
We need a visible point of view on customer success as a revenue function — not just a support function. The argument that churn prevention and expansion are as commercially important as new business generation is still not universally understood, and AI in service is even less well understood than AI in sales or marketing. LinkedIn is the primary distribution channel — both company page and individual voices. Positioning worth owning: why B2B customer success needs the same operational rigour as B2B sales, what AI-powered service actually means in practice, and the cost of reactive support at scale.

**HubSpot Partnership**
As a Diamond Solution Partner, we should actively position ourselves within HubSpot's partner ecosystem as the go-to expert for AI-powered service and customer success implementations. HubSpot's own sales team increasingly leads with Service Hub in customer success conversations — when a HubSpot rep has a customer asking about health scoring, the Customer Agent, or scaling their service operation, we should be the name that comes up. The partner marketplace supports this, but the relationship with the partner team is the deeper value.

**Existing Client Base — Upsell & Cross-Sell**
This is the most natural channel for AI-Powered Service and deserves particular attention. Companies already receiving AI-Powered Sales or AI-Powered Marketing from us already have HubSpot, already trust us, and are almost certainly feeling service pain — growing customer base, stretched team, no health scoring. The conversation is an extension of work already trusted. Every client on a growth retainer is a candidate.

**Referrals & Network**
Complex B2B buyers trust peer recommendations over vendor marketing. Happy clients become the most credible sales channel. Structured referral conversations should be built into the delivery process.

**Outbound — Setting It Up for Ourselves**
We should run AI-Powered Service on our own operation. This means:
- Using the Customer Success Workspace and health scores to monitor our own client base
- Running NPS and CSAT surveys after our own engagements and acting on the results
- Building and maintaining a knowledge base for common client questions about our services
- Using the Customer Agent for routine inbound queries where appropriate

This gives us first-hand proof of what the system produces — and the most credible thing we can bring into a sales conversation is direct experience with the outcome.

## Messaging

**Core message:**
*Your service team is handling more tickets than it can manage, your customers are churning before you see it coming, and your best knowledge lives in your best reps' heads. We build the AI-powered system — on HubSpot — that changes that.*

**Supporting messages by pain point:**

| Pain | Message |
|------|---------|
| Support volume growing faster than the team | "AI resolves 65% of routine tickets autonomously. Your team focuses on what actually requires a human." |
| No visibility into churn risk until the renewal call | "Health scores give every CSM a continuous, automated view of account health — so interventions happen months before the renewal, not the day of." |
| Knowledge locked in experienced reps' heads | "AI builds and maintains a knowledge base from your own tickets. Customers self-serve. New reps get up to speed in days, not months." |
| Service disconnected from the rest of the business | "Because it's built on HubSpot, your service team sees what was promised in the deal — and your sales team sees which accounts are struggling." |
| No consistent feedback loop from customers | "NPS, CSAT, and CES trigger automatically and flow back into the CRM — every response becomes an action, not a spreadsheet row." |
| Customers repeat themselves every time they contact support | "Omnichannel support with full CRM context means the customer never has to explain their situation again." |

**What we are NOT saying:**
- "We implement HubSpot" (too tactical, positions us as a tool vendor)
- "AI replaces your support team" (wrong, and fear-inducing)
- "We'll reduce your headcount" (leads with cost-cutting — the real frame is better service and higher retention, not fewer people)
- "We automate customer success" (customer success is a relationship function; AI supports it, it doesn't run it)

## Sales Motion

The process always starts the same way — with getting the meeting and running discovery. After that, the path splits depending on whether the customer already has HubSpot Service Hub or not.

---

**Step 0: Booking** *(outbound only)*

When we approach a contact who has never heard of us, the goal is to earn a conversation. The outreach has to be relevant enough to be worth their time.

Buying signals that make a service conversation timely:
- A new Head of Customer Success or Support hired (scaling the function = scaling pain)
- The company has recently launched a new product or entered a new market (onboarding challenge incoming)
- The company is in a SaaS or recurring revenue model where churn is existential
- The company recently received funding and is actively growing its customer base
- Job postings for multiple support or CSM roles (volume is outpacing current capacity)

What good outreach at this step looks like:
- Opens with a relevant observation about their business ("I noticed you're hiring three Customer Success Managers — scaling a CS function at that pace usually comes with some operational challenges...")
- Connects it briefly to a problem we solve ("Companies at that stage often find the tooling and process can't keep up with the team...")
- Makes a clear, easy ask ("Would a 20-minute call be worth it?")
- Three to four sentences maximum, sent from a named person

The goal of Step 0 is one thing: get the discovery meeting booked.

---

**Step 1: Discovery** *(always)*

Regardless of how the contact came in — outbound, inbound, referral, or existing client — discovery follows the same logic. We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account, contact records are enriched, and any available signals about their service setup have been reviewed.

The goal is diagnosis, not pitching. We need to understand:
- Their current support setup — what tool, how many tickets, how many people
- Whether there's a dedicated customer success function or whether support handles everything post-sale
- How they currently identify at-risk customers — and whether the honest answer is "we don't, really"
- What their knowledge base situation is — does it exist, who maintains it, do customers actually use it
- What their NPS or CSAT process looks like if any
- What a win would look like for them in the next 6–12 months

The key qualifying question to resolve before the call ends: **do they already have HubSpot Service Hub?** That determines which path follows.

---

*After discovery, the path splits:*

### Path A — New to HubSpot Service Hub (or migrating from a legacy helpdesk)

**2. Proposal**
A scoped proposal covering both the HubSpot Service Hub implementation and the AI-powered service layers built on top of it. These are sold together — the CRM is the foundation the AI depends on, and migrating to HubSpot without building the AI service system on top misses the point. The proposal maps which of the five service functions are in scope and what the full system will look like.

**3. Full implementation**
HubSpot Service Hub is set up as the service and customer success backbone — help desk, knowledge base, customer portal, pipeline, SLA policies — and the AI service system is built on top from the start. Both are delivered as one integrated engagement. Moves to an ongoing retainer for tuning, iteration, and expansion.

---

### Path B — Already on HubSpot Service Hub

Customers already on HubSpot can move directly to a full build, or choose to de-risk the commitment with an audit, a pilot, or both.

**2a. Audit** *(optional)*
A structured assessment of their current Service Hub setup: ticket pipeline configuration, knowledge base quality and coverage, which AI features are active, what's working, what's broken, and what needs to be in place before the AI service system can be properly built on top. Recommended when the Service Hub setup is unclear or has been used only as a shared inbox. Around €3,000–4,000.

**2b. Pilot** *(optional)*
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two service functions where AI will have the most immediate impact. For example: ticket triage and the Breeze Customer Agent, or customer health scoring and the Customer Success Workspace. Recommended when the customer wants to prove value internally before committing to the full system. Around €5,000–6,000.

**3. Build AI-powered service across the full operation**
Roll out the complete system across all five service functions on top of their existing HubSpot setup. Can follow an audit, a pilot, or come directly after discovery if the customer is ready. Moves to an ongoing retainer for monitoring, tuning, and ongoing development.

## What the First Conversation Looks Like

We come prepared: the Company Research Agent has been run on the account and any available signals about their service setup have been reviewed. The goal is diagnosis, not pitching.

**Opening questions to ask:**
- "What does your current support setup look like — are you on HubSpot Service Hub, or something else?"
- "How many support tickets does your team handle per month, and how many people are managing them?"
- "Do you have a dedicated customer success function, or does support handle the full post-sale relationship?"
- "How do you currently know when a customer is at risk of churning — what does that early warning system look like?"
- "What happens to institutional knowledge when an experienced support rep leaves?"

**What to listen for:**
- No HubSpot or legacy helpdesk (Zendesk, Freshdesk, email) → Path A, full implementation
- HubSpot in place but used only as a shared inbox → Path B starting with audit
- Support team overwhelmed by ticket volume → Ticket management and Customer Agent is the hook
- No health scoring or reactive customer success → Customer success workspace and health scoring is the hook
- No knowledge base or outdated documentation → Self-service and Knowledge Base Agent is the hook
- No NPS/CSAT process or feedback going nowhere → Voice of the Customer is the hook
- Service and sales completely disconnected → CRM-native advantage is the hook

**How to make it concrete:**
Walk through a specific scenario — for example, how the Customer Agent works: a customer sends a question about how to configure a feature, the agent finds the answer in the knowledge base, responds within seconds, and the ticket is closed autonomously at €0.50. Or show the health score concept: a customer with two open tickets, no logins in 30 days, and a 6 NPS gets a score of 28/100. Their CSM gets an alert. An intervention happens. The renewal goes ahead. Without the health score, that customer would have been a surprise churn at renewal.

## Pricing Approach

Pricing is not finalised but should reflect the following logic, mapped to the two paths:

**Path A — Full implementation (HubSpot Service Hub + AI-powered service)**
A single project fee covering HubSpot Service Hub setup and AI-powered service configuration together, followed by an ongoing retainer. Priced by scope and which of the five service functions are covered. Around €10,000–15,000.

**Path B — Audit**
A fixed-fee scoping engagement. Produces a clear assessment of the current Service Hub setup and a roadmap for AI-powered service. Priced to be easy to approve. Around €3,000–4,000.

**Path B — Pilot**
A time-boxed engagement (e.g. 90 days) scoped to one or two service functions. Around €5,000–6,000.

**Path B — Full build**
Once the pilot is complete, a project fee for rolling out the remaining functions, followed by an ongoing retainer. Around €10,000–15,000.

**Ongoing retainer (both paths)**
Monthly engagement covering monitoring, knowledge base maintenance, health score tuning, and iteration. The Customer Agent improves as the knowledge base grows. Health scores need refining as more renewal and churn data accumulates. 10–30 hr/month.

**Breeze Customer Agent pricing note**
The Customer Agent runs on HubSpot Credits at €0.50 per resolved conversation. This is a client-side cost, not ours — but it should be modelled in the business case during the sales conversation. For a team handling 500 tickets per month where the agent resolves 65%, the cost is approximately €162/month in credits — typically a fraction of the cost of one additional support hire.

**What to avoid:**
- Pure project pricing with no retainer — the system improves over time and requires active management
- Pricing by tool or feature — this is a service architecture, not a HubSpot add-on
