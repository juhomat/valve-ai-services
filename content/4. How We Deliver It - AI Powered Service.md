# How We Deliver It — AI-Powered Service

## Engagement Model

Every engagement ends up in the same place — an AI-powered service and customer success system running on HubSpot with an ongoing retainer to keep it sharp. How we get there depends on the customer's starting point.

**Path A — Full implementation (new to HubSpot Service Hub or migrating)**
A single integrated project covering HubSpot Service Hub setup and AI-powered service configuration together. Delivered in phases over 6–10 weeks, followed by a monthly retainer. The CRM and Service Hub are the foundation; the AI service system is built on top from day one.

**Path B — Already on HubSpot Service Hub**
Flexible entry points depending on the customer's readiness:
- Audit only → clear assessment of current Service Hub setup and a roadmap, no further commitment required
- Audit + Pilot → prove value on 1–2 service functions before full commitment
- Direct to full build → for customers who are ready and have a solid Service Hub foundation

All paths converge on the same destination: AI-powered service across all five functions — ticket management, self-service, customer success, voice of the customer, and reporting — maintained on a monthly retainer.

**Ongoing retainer**
The system is not a set-and-forget build. The Breeze Customer Agent improves as the knowledge base grows. Health score models need refining as more renewal and churn data accumulates. Survey triggers need reviewing as the customer base evolves. The retainer is what keeps the system performing — typically 10–30 hours per month depending on scope.

## Tools & Technology

**Core platform**
- **HubSpot Service Hub** and **Smart CRM** — the operational backbone. All AI service features run within HubSpot.

**HubSpot AI (Breeze)**
- **Breeze Customer Agent** — autonomous first-line ticket resolution across email, chat, and customer portal; outcome-based pricing at €0.50 per resolved conversation
- **Breeze Knowledge Base Agent** — automatically identifies knowledge gaps from unresolved tickets and drafts new articles for team review
- **Breeze Customer Health Agent** — AI-powered account health assessment with tailored CSM talking points and outreach recommendations (Enterprise, beta)
- **Breeze Assistant** — AI companion for reps and CSMs: ticket summaries, reply drafting assistance, report summaries
- **Breeze Data Agent** — custom questions across full CRM and ticket history for service analytics and reporting
- **Breeze Intelligence** — automatic contact and company enrichment, ensuring customer records are complete before service interactions
- **AI Ticket Triage & Routing** — automatic categorisation, sentiment detection, language detection, and intelligent routing
- **AI Suggested Replies** — real-time response suggestions for reps drawn from the knowledge base and ticket history
- **Ticket Summarisation** — one-click thread summaries for handoffs and escalations
- **Conversation Intelligence** — call recording, transcription, AI analysis, keyword tracking, and coaching insights
- **Customer Health Scores** — continuously updated account health signals aggregated into a single score per account
- **Customer Success Workspace** — purpose-built CSM hub with health scores, alerts, playbooks, and book-of-business view
- **NPS, CSAT & CES surveys** — automated feedback collection with AI sentiment analysis of open-text responses
- **Customer Portal** — branded self-service hub where customers track tickets and access the knowledge base
- **SLA Management** — configurable SLA policies with real-time breach prevention alerts

**Automation**
- **make.com** — for connecting external tools to HubSpot where native integrations don't cover the client's specific needs (e.g. feeding product usage data into health scores, connecting niche industry tools)

**AI development**
- **Claude Code** — used internally for building custom automations, data processing, and bespoke AI logic beyond what HubSpot's native tools provide

## Team & Roles

A typical engagement involves four roles, sometimes carried by two or three people depending on scope:

**Service strategist / consultant**
Owns the service architecture design. Works with the client to define the ticket taxonomy, triage logic, knowledge base structure, health score model, and what "good" looks like across each service function. This person understands the client's customer base and translates it into HubSpot and AI configuration decisions.

**HubSpot specialist**
Owns the technical implementation. Responsible for Service Hub configuration, help desk pipeline setup, SLA policies, omnichannel connections, customer portal, survey setup, and ensuring the HubSpot foundation is solid enough for the AI layer to run on top.

**AI & automation specialist**
Configures the Breeze agents — Customer Agent, Knowledge Base Agent, Customer Health Agent — and sets up health scoring, Conversation Intelligence, make.com flows, and any bespoke automation. Responsible for the quality of the Customer Agent's responses and the integrity of the knowledge base feed.

**Account lead (retainer)**
Owns the ongoing relationship. Monitors Customer Agent resolution rates, knowledge base gap logs, health score accuracy, and survey trends. Manages the monthly retainer hours and brings new HubSpot or AI capabilities to the client's attention as the platform evolves. May be the same person as the strategist.

## Process

### Path A — Full Implementation

**Week 1–2: Kickoff & discovery**
- Kickoff meeting with key stakeholders (service or CS lead, CRM admin, support team representative)
- Deep-dive into current service operation: ticket volume and categories, team structure, existing knowledge base or documentation, customer base profile and contract structure
- Ticket taxonomy workshop: defining the categories, priorities, and routing logic that will govern the triage system
- Access provisioning: HubSpot account, email inboxes, chat, customer portal domain, phone system if applicable
- Review of existing documentation and knowledge assets that will seed the knowledge base

**Week 2–4: HubSpot Service Hub build**
- Help desk configuration: ticket pipeline stages, properties, views, and team structure
- Omnichannel connections: email inboxes, chat widget, customer portal, SMS or WhatsApp where in scope
- SLA policy setup: working hours, SLA targets per ticket type and priority, alert thresholds and notification workflows
- Customer portal setup: branding, access control, knowledge base connection
- Core integrations connected: product usage data where available, billing or contract data, any external tools via make.com

**Week 4–7: AI layer build**
- AI triage rules configured: categorisation logic, sentiment-based priority rules, routing workflows
- Breeze Customer Agent setup: knowledge sources connected (knowledge base, website, documentation), tone and escalation parameters configured, first batch of test conversations run
- Knowledge base built: category architecture designed, first set of foundational articles written from existing documentation and top ticket categories, Knowledge Base Agent activated
- AI suggested replies activated and connected to knowledge base
- Conversation Intelligence enabled: call recording configured, keyword tracking set up for the client's most important themes
- Customer health score designed and built: signals defined and weighted, thresholds set, initial scores validated against known good-fit and at-risk accounts
- Customer Success Workspace configured: book-of-business view set up, health score alerts built, playbooks for at-risk, renewal, and expansion scenarios created
- NPS, CSAT, and CES surveys set up: trigger logic configured, feedback-to-CRM property mapping done, automated follow-up workflows built (detractor alert, promoter referral ask, low CSAT quality review)
- Breeze Data Agent configured: key service analytics questions defined and connected to automated reporting
- Service analytics dashboard built: ticket performance, SLA metrics, Customer Agent resolution rate, agent productivity, feedback trends

**Week 7–8: Testing & training**
- Full system walkthrough with the service and customer success team
- Live test of Customer Agent with real inbound scenarios — review responses together before enabling autonomous mode
- CSM team trained on Customer Success Workspace, health score interpretation, and playbook usage
- Support team trained on help desk, suggested replies, ticket summarisation, and triage workflows
- Final adjustments before go-live

**Week 8+: Go-live & retainer**
- Customer Agent enabled — initially in review mode, moving to autonomous as confidence builds
- System live across all five service functions
- Monthly retainer begins: Customer Agent quality monitoring, knowledge base gap review, health score tuning, survey result review, and iteration

### Path B — Audit

**Week 1: Audit**
- Access to HubSpot Service Hub account
- Assessment of: help desk configuration, ticket pipeline logic, knowledge base quality and coverage, which AI features are active, SLA setup, customer portal status, survey configuration, health scoring if in place
- Output: a clear written assessment and a prioritised roadmap for AI-powered service

**Deliverable:** HubSpot Service Hub audit report + AI-powered service roadmap

---

### Path B — Pilot

**Week 1: Scoping**
- Based on audit findings (or discovery if no audit), agree on which 1–2 service functions are in scope for the pilot
- Define success metrics for the pilot period

**Week 2–3: Build**
- Configure the AI layer for the agreed functions only
- Service and CS team training on the specific tools in scope

**Weeks 3–12: Run & measure**
- System runs for 90 days
- Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins to review Customer Agent resolution rates, knowledge base growth, health score accuracy, or whichever metrics are relevant to the pilot scope
- Results documented against the agreed success metrics

**End of pilot: Review & proposal**
- Pilot debrief with stakeholders
- Results presented against success metrics
- Proposal for full build across remaining functions

---

### Path B — Full Build (post-audit or post-pilot)

Follows the same AI layer build and go-live process as Path A weeks 4–8, scoped to the functions not yet in place.

## Onboarding a New Client

The first two weeks set the tone for the whole engagement. The goal is to move fast enough to show momentum, and thorough enough to build something that actually works.

**Day 1: Access & alignment**
- HubSpot Service Hub access granted to the delivery team
- Email inboxes and chat connected
- Kickoff meeting booked with all key stakeholders
- Delivery timeline shared and agreed

**Week 1: Kickoff meeting**
The kickoff covers:
- The client's service operation end-to-end — how tickets arrive, how they're handled, how long they take, what the common categories are
- The customer success function — how CSMs manage their book of business, how at-risk accounts are identified today, what the renewal process looks like
- Knowledge assets — what documentation exists, where it lives, how current it is
- CRM situation — what's in HubSpot, what's missing, what the service team actually uses
- What success looks like in 90 days

**Week 1–2: Foundations**
- Ticket taxonomy defined and configured: categories, properties, priority levels, routing logic
- Help desk pipeline stages and views set up
- Existing documentation reviewed and catalogued — what can seed the knowledge base immediately
- First knowledge base articles drafted from the most common ticket categories
- Customer Agent connected to initial knowledge sources for first test runs

**First milestone: Customer Agent live on first real tickets**
The first visible output is the Breeze Customer Agent handling real inbound inquiries in review mode — the team sees the responses it would send, approves or adjusts, and builds confidence before enabling autonomous resolution. This is typically achievable within 2–3 weeks of kickoff and gives the client an immediate, tangible signal that the system is working — not just configuration happening in the background.
