# Who Buys It — AI-Powered Marketing

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Growth-Oriented Mid-Market — €30–100M revenue**

Industrial, deep-tech, or B2B technology companies with:

- **50–500 employees**
- **International growth ambitions** — operating or expanding across 2–5 markets
- **A marketing owner or small marketing team** — at least one person responsible for marketing with the mandate to build it properly
- **A sales team already in place** — marketing exists to generate and support pipeline, not to replace sales
- **6–18 month sales cycles** — complex deals with multiple stakeholders, where content, reputation, and buyer nurturing matter
- Entering a **next growth phase** — scaling into new markets, launching new products, or trying to generate more pipeline without proportionally increasing the team
- **HubSpot already in place** OR actively migrating from another platform

**Why this profile is the right fit:**
- Large enough to invest in a proper marketing system and see meaningful ROI
- Small enough that the marketing team is stretched — they need to produce more with the same people, which is exactly what AI-powered marketing enables
- Complex enough offerings that content, education, and reputation do serious commercial work
- High potential impact from structured CRM data, AI-powered content pipelines, and proper attribution

**Example customer types:**
- Industrial tech companies building brand and demand generation in international markets
- Climate and energy scale-ups trying to explain complex products to technical buyers
- Professional services firms turning expertise into thought leadership that generates inbound pipeline
- B2B SaaS companies moving upmarket and needing marketing that reflects that positioning

**Named examples from our client base:** Stora Enso, Virta, Abax, Katana

**Two types of buyer — same ICP, different starting point:**
- **Already on HubSpot** — Marketing Hub is in place but being used as a basic email tool. AI capabilities are unused, data is messy, and content production is still manual. The engagement builds the marketing system on top of what exists.
- **New to HubSpot or migrating** — the platform move and the AI-powered marketing build happen together as one integrated engagement. Migrating off legacy tools or off disconnected stacks (Mailchimp + WordPress + spreadsheets) is part of the work.

The sales motion differs between the two (see How We Sell It), but the ICP profile is the same.

## The Pain They Feel

These companies have marketing activity — but it's manual, fragmented, and hard to connect to results. Common pain points that make them ready for AI-Powered Marketing:

- **Content production is slow and doesn't scale** — a small team is trying to produce blog posts, emails, social content, and campaign materials, and everything takes too long. Output is inconsistent and the calendar is always behind.
- **The CRM data is too messy to use** — contacts have missing fields, duplicates, no enrichment. Segmentation attempts produce lists that don't reflect reality. Marketing can't personalise because the data isn't there.
- **No clarity on what's actually driving pipeline** — marketing is running campaigns but can't prove which activities generated which deals. The board asks about ROI and the answer is a collection of vanity metrics.
- **Everyone gets the same message** — emails go to the full list, content isn't adapted for different audience segments, and personalisation is either absent or superficial.
- **Social presence is ad hoc** — posts go out when someone has time, not as part of a coordinated system. Channels are underutilised or managed reactively.
- **No visibility in AI search** — buyers are asking ChatGPT and Perplexity questions about their category, and the company isn't appearing. Competitors are getting cited instead.
- **Current CRM or marketing platform isn't working** — they're on a disconnected stack or a legacy system that the team has stopped trusting. They want a platform that actually centralises everything and a partner who can build on it properly. *(Path A buyers)*
- **Marketing and sales are misaligned** — leads are generated but sales doesn't act on them because quality is unclear, hand-off is manual, or the two teams are working from different data.
- **No competitive intelligence** — the team doesn't have a systematic way to track what competitors are doing, how the market is talking about them, or where the brand stands relative to alternatives.

## Who Is NOT a Good Fit

**We say no to:**
- Companies with **< €10M revenue and no commercial structure** — the ROI isn't there and the foundation isn't ready
- **Marketing teams that don't exist** — if there's no one inside the company who owns marketing with real mandate, there's no one to operate the system we build. AI amplifies an existing function; it can't replace one that doesn't exist.
- **"Just run our social media" or "write our content" expectations** — AI-Powered Marketing is a system-level engagement. We set up and tune the infrastructure that produces content at scale; we are not a content production agency on retainer.
- **"Just get us more leads quickly"** — the Loop Marketing framework is designed for compounding results over cycles, not instant pipeline. Buyers expecting immediate returns from a one-off campaign won't get what they're looking for here.
- Companies with **no CRM commitment** or a history of tool-hopping without strategy — the entire service depends on HubSpot as the operational backbone. If there's no commitment to that, the engagement doesn't hold together.
- **Purely price-driven buyers** — this work requires strategic commitment and partnership, not the cheapest option.

**Why this matters:** Bad-fit customers drain resources, reduce profitability, block scalability, and distract from the work that actually drives strategic growth.
